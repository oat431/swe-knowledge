# Thai-English Common Mistakes

These errors come from the structural differences between Thai and English. Knowing them is half the battle — awareness lets you catch yourself before the mistake leaves your mouth (or keyboard).

---

## 1. Missing Articles (A/An/The)

Thai has no articles. English requires them.

| ❌ Error | ✅ Correct | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| I bought car. | I bought **a** car. | First mention, non-specific |
| Sun is hot. | **The** sun is hot. | Unique thing |
| She is teacher. | She is **a** teacher. | Job/profession needs article |

> See [[Articles]] for full detail.

---

## 2. No Tense Conjugation

Thai verbs don't change for tense — time is shown by context words (เมื่อวาน, จะ, กำลัง, แล้ว). English requires the verb itself to change.

| ❌ Error | ✅ Correct |
|---------|-----------|
| Yesterday I go to the market. | Yesterday I **went** to the market. |
| She work here since 2020. | She **has worked** here since 2020. |
| I eat already. | I **have eaten** already. |
| Tomorrow I will went. | Tomorrow I **will go**. (V1 after will!) |

> See [[12 English Tense]] for the full system.

---

## 3. Subject Omission

Thai drops subjects when context is clear. English demands them — nearly every sentence needs a subject.

| ❌ Error | ✅ Correct |
|---------|-----------|
| Is raining. | **It** is raining. |
| Is 5 o'clock. | **It** is 5 o'clock. |
| Have 3 people. | **There** are 3 people. / **We** have 3 people. |

> English uses **dummy subjects** — "it" and "there" — when no real subject exists. Thai doesn't need them.

---

## 4. "And" Overuse (Run-on Sentences)

Thai uses `และ` (láe) and `แล้วก็` loosely. English uses sentence boundaries and varied connectors.

| ❌ Error | ✅ Correct |
|---------|-----------|
| I woke up and ate breakfast and went to school and met my friend and we talked. | I woke up and ate breakfast. **Then** I went to school, **where** I met my friend. We talked for a while. |

> See [[Sentence Structure]] and [[Conjunctions]].

---

## 5. Double Connectors

Thai pairs connectors naturally: `เพราะว่า...ก็เลย` (because...so), `ถึงแม้ว่า...แต่ก็` (although...but). English forbids this.

| ❌ Error | ✅ Correct |
|---------|-----------|
| **Because** it rained, **so** I stayed home. | **Because** it rained, I stayed home. / It rained, **so** I stayed home. |
| **Although** he's rich, **but** he's unhappy. | **Although** he's rich, he's unhappy. / He's rich, **but** he's unhappy. |

> **One connector per clause relationship.**

---

## 6. Missing "Be" Before Adjectives

Thai adjectives can function as verbs — `เขาสูง` (literally "he tall"). English needs `be`.

| ❌ Error | ✅ Correct |
|---------|-----------|
| She beautiful. | She **is** beautiful. |
| I hungry. | I **am** hungry. |
| The food delicious. | The food **is** delicious. |

---

## 7. Wrong Word Order in Questions

Thai question particles go at the end and word order doesn't change. English inverts subject and auxiliary.

| ❌ Error | ✅ Correct |
|---------|-----------|
| You like coffee? | **Do** you like coffee? |
| He went where? | **Where did** he go? |
| She is how old? | **How old is** she? |

> See [[Question Formation]].

---

## 8. Plural Missing

Thai doesn't mark plurals on nouns. English adds -s/-es (with exceptions).

| ❌ Error | ✅ Correct |
|---------|-----------|
| I have two dog. | I have two **dogs**. |
| Many student came. | Many **students** came. |
| Information**s** (uncountable!) | Information / a piece of information |

---

## 9. Direct Translation of Thai Idioms

| Thai Expression | Literal Translation (❌) | Natural English (✅) |
|----------------|------------------------|---------------------|
| เข้าใจไหม? | Do you understand? (OK) / Enter heart? (❌) | **Got it?** / **Does that make sense?** |
| สู้ๆ | Fight fight! | **Hang in there!** / **You can do it!** |
| ช่างมัน | Let it be. / Never mind. | **Whatever.** / **Forget it.** |
| เกรงใจ | (no word in English) | **I don't want to impose.** / **I feel awkward asking.** |

---

## Self-Check Questions

Before speaking/writing, ask yourself:
1. Does this verb need tense? (When did it happen?)
2. Does this noun need an article? (a/an/the/none?)
3. Is the subject there? (Who/what is doing this?)
4. Did I use one connector where Thai uses two?
5. Is the word order right for a question?
