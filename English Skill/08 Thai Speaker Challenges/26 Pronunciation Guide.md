# Pronunciation Guide (Thai Speakers)

English has sounds that don't exist in Thai. Mastering these makes the difference between being understood and being misunderstood.

---

## Problem Sounds

### 1. /θ/ (TH — voiceless) as in "**th**ink"

Put your tongue between your teeth and blow air. No vibration.

| ✅ | ❌ (common error) |
|---|-------------------|
| **th**ink | sink / tink / fink |
| **th**ank you | tank you / sank you |
| **th**ree | tree / sree / free |

> Practice: "I **think** I need **three** **things**."

### 2. /ð/ (TH — voiced) as in "**th**is"

Same tongue position as /θ/, but vibrate your vocal cords.

| ✅ | ❌ |
|---|-----|
| **th**is | dis / zis |
| **th**at | dat |
| mo**th**er | moder |

> Practice: "**This** is my mo**th**er and **th**at is my fa**th**er."

### 3. /ʃ/ (SH) as in "**sh**e"

Round your lips and push air through. Don't make it a "ch" sound.

| ✅ | ❌ |
|---|-----|
| **sh**e | chee / see |
| **sh**op | chop / sop |
| fini**sh** | finich / finis |

### 4. /ʒ/ (ZH) as in "televi**s**ion"

Same mouth as /ʃ/, but vibrate. Rare in English but appears in common words.

| ✅ | ❌ |
|---|-----|
| u**s**ually | you-sually (hard s) |
| televi**s**ion | televishun |
| gara**g**e | garadge (hard g) |

### 5. /r/ — The English R

NOT a rolling/flapping R like Thai. Curl your tongue back without touching the roof.

| ✅ | ❌ |
|---|-----|
| **r**ed | led (confusing with L) |
| **r**ight | light |
| ca**r** | cah (dropping R entirely) |

> Practice: "**R**ed **r**oses **r**un **r**ound the **r**oad."

### 6. /l/ vs /r/ — The Classic Confusion

| Word Pair | Meaning Difference |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **l**ight / **r**ight | ความสว่าง / ถูกต้อง |
| **l**ead / **r**ead | นำ / อ่าน |
| co**l**lect / co**r**rect | เก็บรวบรวม / ถูกต้อง |
| g**l**ass / g**r**ass | แก้ว / หญ้า |

> /l/: Tongue tip touches the roof behind teeth. /r/: Tongue curls back, no contact.

### 7. Final Consonants — DON'T Drop Them!

Thai often drops or glottalizes final consonants. English pronounces them clearly.

| Word | ❌ (dropping final) | ✅ (pronounce the ending) |
|------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| **rice** | rye (ไร) | rai**s** |
| **right** | rye (ไร) | rai**t** |
| **ride** | rye (ไร) | rai**d** |
| **ripe** | rye (ไร) | rai**p** |

> **Rice ≠ right ≠ ride ≠ ripe.** If you drop the final consonant, all four sound the same.

### 8. Consonant Clusters

Thai rarely has multiple consonants together at the start or end. English does.

| ❌ (Thai pattern: insert vowel) | ✅ (English: cluster directly) |
|-------------------------------|------------------------------|
| **suh**-chool (school) | **s**chool (no vowel between s and k) |
| **suh**-tart (start) | **st**art |
| tex**-uh** (texts) | te**xts** (pronounce the whole cluster) |

## Stress & Intonation

### Word Stress

English stresses certain syllables — wrong stress makes you hard to understand.

| Word | ❌ Wrong Stress | ✅ Right Stress |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| **DE**velop | de**VE**lop | **DE**velop |
| **PHO**tograph | pho**TO**graph | **PHO**tograph |
| pho**TO**grapher | **PHO**tographer | pho**TO**grapher |

### Sentence Stress

English stresses content words (nouns, verbs, adjectives) and reduces function words (articles, prepositions, auxiliaries).

> "I want to **GO** to the **STORE**." (NOT "I **WANT** **TO** **GO** **TO** **THE** **STORE**")

## ⚠️ Common Mispronunciations

| Word | ❌ | ✅ |
|------|---|-----|
| **vegetable** | ve-ge-ta-ble | **vej**-tuh-bull (3 syllables) |
| **comfortable** | com-for-ta-ble | **cumf**-tuh-bull (3 syllables) |
| **island** | is-land | **eye**-lund (silent s) |
| **climb** | climb (pronounce b) | **clime** (silent b) |
| **receipt** | re-seept | re-**seet** (silent p) |
| **salmon** | sal-mon | **sam**-un (silent l) |
