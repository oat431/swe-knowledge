# False Friends & Direct Translation Pitfalls

False friends are words that look similar in Thai and English but mean different things — or Thai expressions that don't translate directly. Direct translation is the #1 source of unnatural English.

---

## False Friends — Similar Sound, Different Meaning

| Thai Word | Sounds Like... | But Means... | ❌ Wrong Usage | ✅ Correct |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|---------------|-----------|
| เช็ค (check) | check (verify) | Also: bill (restaurant) | Can I have the check? | Can I have the **bill**? (UK) / **check** (US — both OK in US) |
| ฟรี (free) | free (no cost) | Same — but overused | Free gift, free service | **Complimentary** (more natural in formal contexts) |
| แอร์ (air) | air | Air conditioner | Turn on the air. | Turn on the **air conditioning** / **AC**. |
| เวอร์ (over) | over | Exaggerated, over the top | That's so over! | That's **over the top**! |

## Direct Translation Traps

### 1. "Same Same"

Thai: `เหมือนๆ กัน` — natural in Thai, sounds childish/redundant in English.

| ❌ | ✅ |
|---|-----|
| These two are same same. | These two are **the same**. / **similar**. / **identical**. |

### 2. "I go to..."

Thai uses `ไป` (bpai) for many movement scenarios. English uses specific verbs.

| ❌ | ✅ |
|---|-----|
| I go to the toilet. | I **need to use** the restroom. / I'm going to the bathroom. |
| I go to eat. | I'm **going to get** something to eat. |

### 3. "Do you know...?" vs "Do you understand...?"

| Thai | ❌ | ✅ |
|------|---|---|
| รู้ไหม? | Do you know? (when you mean understand) | **Do you understand?** / **Got it?** |
| เข้าใจไหม? | Enter heart? | **Do you understand?** |

> "Do you know?" = Do you have the information? "Do you understand?" = Do you comprehend?

### 4. "I don't sure" (ไม่แน่ใจ)

| ❌ | ✅ |
|---|-----|
| I don't sure. | I'm **not sure**. |
| I don't certain. | I'm **not certain**. |

> "Sure" and "certain" are adjectives — they need "be" (am/is/are), not "do."

### 5. "Can you...?" vs Politeness

Thai uses `ขอ` (khǎw) for polite requests. English uses "Could" or "Would" — "Can" sounds demanding.

| ❌ (too direct) | ✅ (polite) |
|----------------|------------|
| Can you give me water? | **Could** I have some water? |
| I want coffee. | **I'd like** a coffee, please. / **Could I get** a coffee? |

### 6. "I have ever..." (ฉันเคย)

Thai: `เคย` (khoei) = have ever. In English, "ever" is mostly for questions.

| ❌ | ✅ |
|---|-----|
| I have ever been to Japan. | I **have been** to Japan. |
| Have you ever been to Japan? | ✅ Correct! ("ever" OK in questions) |

### 7. "Already" Overuse

Thai uses `แล้ว` (láew) constantly. English "already" is only for emphasis that something happened sooner than expected.

| ❌ (unnatural) | ✅ (natural) |
|---------------|-------------|
| I ate already. | I **ate**. / I've **eaten**. |
| I finished already. (fine, but overused) | I **finished**. |

"Already" = เร็วเกินคาด (sooner than expected), not just "แล้ว" (completed).

### 8. "Play" for Everything (เล่น)

Thai `เล่น` covers many activities. English uses specific verbs.

| ❌ | ✅ |
|---|-----|
| Let's play internet. | Let's **go online** / **surf the web**. |
| I play phone. | I'm **on my phone**. / I'm **using** my phone. |
| Play music. (correct!) | ✅ This one works. |

### 9. "Very" Overuse

Thai มาก (mâak) → English "very." But English has stronger, more specific words.

| ❌ (weak) | ✅ (stronger) |
|----------|--------------|
| very big | **huge**, **enormous** |
| very small | **tiny**, **minute** |
| very good | **excellent**, **outstanding** |
| very bad | **terrible**, **awful** |
| very happy | **thrilled**, **delighted** |
| very tired | **exhausted** |

## Quick Reference: Common Traps at a Glance

| Thai Expression | ❌ Direct Translation | ✅ Natural English |
|----------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| ไม่เป็นไร | Never mind. / It's nothing. | **No problem.** / **It's fine.** / **Don't worry about it.** |
| เกรงใจ | (no translation) | **I don't want to impose.** / **I feel bad asking.** |
| ขอโทษ | Sorry. / Excuse me. | **Sorry** (apology). **Excuse me** (getting attention/passing by). |
| เมื่อกี้ | Just now | **A moment ago.** / **Just a second ago.** |
| เดี๋ยว | Wait / In a moment | **Just a moment.** / **Hold on.** / **I'll do it soon.** |
| นิดหน่อย | A little bit | **A little.** / **A bit.** (not "a little bit" for everything) |
| สบายๆ | Comfortable | **Take it easy.** / **I'm good.** / **Whatever's convenient.** |
