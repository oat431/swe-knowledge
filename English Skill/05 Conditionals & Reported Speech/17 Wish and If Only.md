# Wish and If Only

We use `wish` and `if only` to express desires for things that are NOT true — regrets about the present, past, or hopes for change. The grammar follows conditional patterns.

---

## Wish vs If Only

| Expression | Meaning | Intensity |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **I wish...** | I want something different from reality | Normal |
| **If only...** | Same meaning, but stronger emotion | Stronger — regret, frustration |

> "I wish I had more money." = normal desire
> "If only I had more money!" = stronger — frustration, longing

---

## Wish + Past Simple — Unreal Present

> **I wish + Past Simple**

For things you want to be different NOW. (Parallels Second Conditional.)

| Wish | Reality |
|------|---------|
| I wish I **had** a car. | I don't have a car. |
| I wish I **knew** the answer. | I don't know the answer. |
| I wish it **wasn't raining**. | It is raining. |
| She wishes she **lived** near the beach. | She doesn't live near the beach. |

⚠️ **"I wish I were"** — same subjunctive rule as conditionals:
> I wish I **were** taller. ✅

---

## Wish + Past Perfect — Regret About the Past

> **I wish + Past Perfect (had + V3)**

For things you regret about the PAST — things that happened or didn't happen. (Parallels Third Conditional.)

| Wish | Reality |
|------|---------|
| I wish I **had studied** harder. | I didn't study hard. |
| I wish I **hadn't said** that. | I said it, and I regret it. |
| She wishes she **had taken** the job. | She didn't take the job. |
| I wish you **had told** me. | You didn't tell me. |

---

## Wish + Would — Desire for Change (Complaint)

> **I wish + would + V1**

For things you want to change — often about other people's behavior, or situations you're annoyed about. You CANNOT use this about yourself.

| Wish | Situation |
|------|-----------|
| I wish you **would listen**. | You don't listen — I want you to. |
| I wish it **would stop** raining. | It keeps raining — I'm annoyed. |
| I wish he **wouldn't smoke** in the house. | He smokes; I want him to stop. |
| I wish the bus **would come**. | I'm waiting impatiently. |

> ❌ "I wish I would..." (wrong — can't use about yourself)

---

## If Only — Same Rules, Stronger Emotion

| If Only | Same As |
|---------|---------|
| If only I **had** more time. | I wish I had more time. |
| If only I **had known**! | I wish I had known. (strong regret) |
| If only he **would call**! | I wish he would call. (frustration) |

---

## Summary Table

| Wish Structure | About | Reality | Conditional Parallel |
|---------------|-------|---------|---------------------|
| wish + **Past Simple** | Present situation | Not true now | Second Conditional |
| wish + **Past Perfect** | Past event | Didn't happen | Third Conditional |
| wish + **would** | Future change / complaint | Someone/something won't change | — |

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai uses `อยากให้` (yàak hâi) or `น่าจะ` — no tense shift.

| Thai | ❌ | ✅ |
|------|---|---|
| ฉันอยากให้ฝนหยุดตก | I wish it stops raining. | I wish it **would stop** raining. |
| อยากให้ฉันรู้มากกว่านี้ | I wish I know more. | I wish I **knew** more. |
| น่าจะเรียนให้มากกว่านี้ | I wish I study more. | I wish I **had studied** more. |
