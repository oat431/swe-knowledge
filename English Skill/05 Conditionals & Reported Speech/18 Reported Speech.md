# Reported Speech (Indirect Speech)

Reported speech is how we tell someone what another person said — without quoting them word-for-word. It requires shifting tenses, pronouns, and time expressions backward.

---

## Direct vs Reported

| Direct Speech (exact words) | Reported Speech (paraphrased) |
|---------------------------|------------------------------|
| She said, "**I am** tired." | She said (that) she **was** tired. |
| He said, "**I will call** you." | He said he **would call** me. |
| "**Where do** you live?" she asked. | She asked **where I lived**. |

---

## The Backshift Rule

When the reporting verb is **past** (said, told, asked), the tense shifts BACK one step:

| Direct Speech | → | Reported Speech |
|--------------|---|-----------------|
| Present Simple → | | Past Simple |
| Present Continuous → | | Past Continuous |
| Past Simple → | | Past Perfect |
| Present Perfect → | | Past Perfect |
| will → | | would |
| can → | | could |
| may → | | might |
| must → | | had to |

### Examples

| Direct | Reported |
|--------|----------|
| "I **like** coffee." | She said she **liked** coffee. |
| "I **am working**." | He said he **was working**. |
| "I **went** to Paris." | She said she **had gone** to Paris. |
| "I **have finished**." | He said he **had finished**. |
| "I **will help**." | She said she **would help**. |
| "I **can swim**." | He said he **could swim**. |

---

## When NOT to Backshift

### 1. Reporting verb is present/future
> She **says** she **likes** coffee. (no backshift — "says" is present)

### 2. Still true / General truth
> "The earth is round." → He said the earth **is** round. (still true)
> "I'm 25 years old." → She said she **is** 25. (but "was 25" also OK if time passed)

### 3. Past Perfect / Modals with no past form
> "I **had already eaten**." → He said he **had already eaten**. (no further backshift possible)
> "I **should go**." → He said he **should go**. (should → should, no change)

---

## Pronoun and Time Expression Shifts

| Direct | → | Reported |
|--------|---|----------|
| **I / we** | → | he/she / they |
| **my / our** | → | his/her / their |
| **now** | → | then / at that moment |
| **today** | → | that day |
| **yesterday** | → | the day before / the previous day |
| **tomorrow** | → | the next day / the following day |
| **this (week/month)** | → | that (week/month) |
| **here** | → | there |
| **ago** | → | before / earlier |

> "I'll see you **tomorrow**." → He said he would see me **the next day**.

---

## Say vs Tell

| Verb | Pattern | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **say** | say (that)... / say **to** someone | He **said** (that) he was tired. / He said **to** me... |
| **tell** | tell **someone** (that)... | He **told me** (that) he was tired. |

> ❌ "He said me..." ✅ "He **told** me..." or "He **said to** me..."

---

## Reported Questions

Word order changes: question form → statement form. No `do/does/did` in reported questions.

| Direct Question | Reported Question |
|----------------|-------------------|
| "Where **do you** live?" | She asked where **I lived**. |
| "What **is** your name?" | He asked what **my name was**. |
| "**Did** you **see** the movie?" | She asked **if/whether** I **had seen** the movie. |
| "**Can** you help?" | He asked **if** I **could help**. |

### Wh- Questions → Keep the Wh- word
> "Where are you going?" → She asked where I was going.

### Yes/No Questions → Use "if" or "whether"
> "Do you like it?" → He asked **if** I liked it.

---

## Reported Commands / Requests

> tell/ask + person + **(not) to V1**

| Direct | Reported |
|--------|----------|
| "Sit down." | He told me **to sit** down. |
| "Don't be late." | She told me **not to be** late. |
| "Please help me." | He asked me **to help** him. |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai has no tense system, so backshifting feels unnatural. The instinct is to keep the original tense.

| Thai Pattern | ❌ | ✅ |
|-------------|------|-----|
| เขาบอกว่าเขาชอบกาแฟ | He said he likes coffee. | He said he **liked** coffee. |
| เธอถามว่าฉันไปไหน | She asked where do I go. | She asked **where I went**. |
| เขาบอกว่าเขาจะโทรมา | He said he will call. | He said he **would call**. |
