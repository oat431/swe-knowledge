# Word Families

One root word can produce many forms. Knowing the family lets you switch between noun, verb, adjective, and adverb — essential for IELTS/TOEFL where you need to paraphrase.

---

## Why Word Families Matter

| Situation | Problem | Solution |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| IELTS Reading | The passage says "analyse" but the question says "analysis" | Know they're the same family |
| TOEFL Writing | You keep repeating "important" | Switch to "significance," "significantly," "signify" |
| IELTS Speaking | You can't think of the right form | Know all forms and pick the one that fits |

---

## How to Build Word Families

Most English words change form with **prefixes** and **suffixes**:

```
         PREFIX + ROOT + SUFFIX
         ─────   ────   ──────
         un-    happy   -ness
                   ↓
              unhappiness
```

## The 20 Most Important Word Families for IELTS/TOEFL

### 1. ANALYSE

| Form | Part of Speech | Example |
|------|---------------|---------|
| **analyse** (v) | verb | We need to **analyse** the results. |
| **analysis** (n) | noun | The **analysis** revealed a pattern. |
| **analyst** (n) | noun | The **analyst** reviewed the data. |
| **analytical** (adj) | adjective | She has strong **analytical** skills. |
| **analytically** (adv) | adverb | Think **analytically** about the problem. |

### 2. SIGNIFY

| Form | Part of Speech | Example |
|------|---------------|---------|
| **signify** (v) | verb | What does this **signify**? |
| **significance** (n) | noun | The **significance** of this finding is huge. |
| **significant** (adj) | adjective | There was a **significant** increase. |
| **significantly** (adv) | adverb | Prices rose **significantly**. |
| **insignificant** (adj) | adjective | The change was **insignificant**. |

### 3. ECONOMY

| Form | Part of Speech | Example |
|------|---------------|---------|
| **economy** (n) | noun | The **economy** is recovering. |

| **economical** (adj) | adjective | This car is very **economical**. ( = efficient) |
| **economically** (adv) | adverb | The plan is **economically** viable. |
| **economist** (n) | noun | The **economist** predicted a recession. |

## Common Suffixes Cheat Sheet

| Suffix | Part of Speech | Examples |
|--------|---------------|----------|
| **-tion / -sion / -ation** | noun | education, decision, variation |
| **-ment** | noun | achievement, development, environment |
| **-ness** | noun | awareness, happiness, effectiveness |
| **-ity / -ty** | noun | diversity, complexity, security |
| **-ance / -ence** | noun | significance, evidence, occurrence |
| **-er / -or** | noun (person) | researcher, analyst, educator |
| **-ive / -ative** | adjective | creative, analytical, significant |
| **-al / -ial** | adjective | environmental, economic, traditional |
| **-able / -ible** | adjective | achievable, identifiable, accessible |
| **-ly** | adverb | significantly, analytically, economically |
| **-ize / -ise** | verb | analyse, categorize, summarize |
| **-ify** | verb | signify, identify, classify |

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| Trap | Example | Fix |
|------|---------|-----|
| Using wrong form | "The **analyse** shows..." | "The **analysis** shows..." (noun needed) |
| Confusing -ic and -ical | "economical growth" | "**Economic** growth" (economical = efficient) |
| Forgetting word families exist | Repeating "important" 5 times | Use: significance, significantly, signify |
| Adding wrong suffix | "significanceness" | Not all combinations exist — check! |
