# Causative Verbs

Causative verbs express that someone **causes** something to happen — but they may or may not do it themselves. The core four are: **make, have, let, get** (plus **help**).

---

## The Big Four

| Verb | Meaning | Structure | Example |
|------|---------|-----------|---------|
| **make** | Force someone to do something | make + person + **V1** | She **made** me **apologize**. |
| **have** | Arrange for someone to do something | have + person + **V1** | I **had** the mechanic **fix** the car. |
| **let** | Allow someone to do something | let + person + **V1** | My parents **let** me **stay** out late. |
| **get** | Persuade someone to do something | get + person + **to V1** | I **got** her **to help** me. |

### The Pattern Difference

| Verb | Pattern | Correct | Wrong |
|------|---------|---------|-------|
| make | + V1 (no "to") | She made me **go**. | She made me **to go**. ❌ |
| have | + V1 (no "to") | I had him **check** it. | I had him **to check** it. ❌ |
| let | + V1 (no "to") | Let me **help**. | Let me **to help**. ❌ |
| get | + **to** V1 | I got her **to agree**. | I got her agree. ❌ |

---

## Detailed Usage

### Make — Force or Cause

> make + person + **V1** (no "to")

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| The teacher **made** the students **rewrite** the test. | Forced them |
| Sad movies **make** me **cry**. | Cause an involuntary reaction |
| He **made** me **feel** stupid. | Caused an emotional state |

⚠️ In passive voice, "make" takes "to":
> I **was made to apologize**. (passive — "to" appears)

### Have — Arrange or Authorize

> have + person + **V1** (no "to")

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| I **had** the barber **cut** my hair. | I arranged it; barber did it |
| The manager **had** the team **prepare** a report. | Delegated |
| I'll **have** my assistant **call** you. | Will arrange |

### Have/Get + Object + V3 (Passive Causative)

When you arrange for something to be done **to** an object:

> have/get + object + **V3**

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| I **had** my hair **cut**. | Someone cut it for me |
| I **got** my car **fixed**. | I arranged the repair |
| We're **having** the house **painted**. | Arranging painters |
| She **got** her phone **repaired**. | She arranged it |

> "I cut my hair." = I did it myself. "I had my hair cut." = someone else did it.

### Let — Allow / Permit

> let + person + **V1** (no "to")

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| My boss **let** me **leave** early. | Allowed |
| **Let** me **know** if you need help. | Common phrase |
| Don't **let** him **get** away with that. | Allow |
| She won't **let** her children **watch** TV. | Doesn't allow |

### Get — Persuade / Convince

> get + person + **to V1** (with "to"!)

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| I **got** my brother **to lend** me money. | Persuaded him |
| How did you **get** her **to agree**? | Convinced |
| I can't **get** the children **to sleep**. | Can't make them |

---

## Help — The Flexible One

**Help** can take either V1 or to V1 — both are correct:

| Both Correct | |
|-------------|---|
| She **helped** me **carry** the bags. ✅ | |
| She **helped** me **to carry** the bags. ✅ | |

> V1 (without "to") is more common in everyday speech.

---

## Quick Comparison

| Verb | Force Level | Structure |
|------|------------|-----------|
| **make** | Maximum (force) | make + person + V1 |
| **get** | Medium (persuade) | get + person + **to** V1 |
| **have** | Medium (arrange/authority) | have + person + V1 |
| **let** | Minimum (permit) | let + person + V1 |
| **help** | Supportive | help + person + (to) V1 |

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai uses `ให้` (hâi) for many causative meanings — one word covers make/have/let/get.

| Thai | ❌ | ✅ |
|------|---|---|
| ฉันให้เขาทำ | I let/make/have him do. | Depends on meaning! |
| ครูให้ฉันเขียนใหม่ | Teacher let me rewrite it. | Teacher **made** me rewrite it. (force) |
| ฉันให้ช่างซ่อมรถ | I let the mechanic fix the car. | I **had** the mechanic fix the car. (arrange) |

> **Don't use `ให้` as a crutch.** Think about the relationship: are you forcing (make), arranging (have), permitting (let), or persuading (get)?
