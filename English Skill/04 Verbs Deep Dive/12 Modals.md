# Modals

Modal verbs express ability, possibility, permission, obligation, and advice. They don't change form (no -s, no -ing, no -ed) and are always followed by a **bare infinitive** (V1 without "to").

---

## The Core Modals

| Modal | Primary Meaning | Strength | Example |
|-------|----------------|----------|---------|
| **can** | Ability, permission | Strong | I **can** swim. |
| **could** | Past ability, polite request, possibility | Softer | **Could** you help me? |
| **may** | Permission, possibility | Formal | You **may** leave. |
| **might** | Weak possibility | Weakest | It **might** rain. |
| **must** | Obligation, strong certainty | Strongest | You **must** stop. |
| **have to** | External obligation | Strong | I **have to** go to work. |
| **should** | Advice, expectation | Medium | You **should** see a doctor. |
| **will** | Future, willingness | Strong | I **will** help you. |
| **would** | Hypothetical, polite | Softer | I **would** like coffee. |
| **shall** | Suggestion (formal, British) | Formal | **Shall** we begin? |

## Modals by Function

### Ability

| Modal | Time | Example |
|-------|------|---------|
| **can** | Present | I **can** speak English. |
| **could** | Past general ability | I **could** run fast when I was young. |
| **was/were able to** | Past specific success | I **was able to** fix it yesterday. ✅ (NOT "I could fix it yesterday" for a specific event) |

> ⚠️ "I could fix it yesterday" implies you had the ability but didn't necessarily do it. "I was able to fix it" = you successfully did it.

### Permission

| Modal | Formality | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **can** | Informal | **Can** I borrow your pen? |
| **could** | Polite | **Could** I use your phone? |
| **may** | Formal | **May** I come in? |

### Obligation / Necessity

| Modal | Strength | Source | Example |
|-------|----------|--------|---------|
| **must** | Strong | Internal (speaker's authority) | I **must** remember to call her. |
| **have to** | Strong | External (rules, circumstances) | You **have to** wear a seatbelt. |
| **should** | Medium | Advice | You **should** exercise more. |
| **ought to** | Medium (less common) | Moral duty | You **ought to** apologize. |

### Prohibition

| Modal | Example |
|-------|---------|
| **must not / mustn't** | You **mustn't** smoke here. (= forbidden) |
| **cannot / can't** | You **can't** park here. |
| **may not** | You **may not** enter. (formal) |

### Lack of Necessity vs Prohibition

| Expression | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **don't have to** | Not necessary (but allowed) |
| **mustn't** | Forbidden (not allowed) |

> "You **don't have to** come." = it's optional, you can come or not.
> "You **mustn't** come." = stay away, you are not allowed.

### Certainty / Deduction

| Modal | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **must** | 95% sure (present) | She **must be** at work. (It's 9am, her car is gone) |
| **can't** | 99% sure it's impossible | That **can't be** true. |
| **may / might / could** | 50% or less | He **might be** sick. (I'm not sure) |
| **must have + V3** | 95% sure (past) | She **must have left** early. |
| **might have + V3** | Possible past | He **might have forgotten**. |

---

## Modal + Perfect (Past Modals)

| Structure | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **should have + V3** | Regret — right thing wasn't done | I **should have studied** harder. |
| **could have + V3** | Past possibility not taken | You **could have died**! |
| **would have + V3** | Hypothetical past | I **would have called**, but I lost your number. |
| **must have + V3** | Almost certain about past | She **must have been** tired. |
| **might have + V3** | Possible past | He **might have missed** the bus. |

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| Thai | ❌ | ✅ |
|------|---|---|
| อาจจะฝนตก | Maybe it rain. | It **might** rain. |
| คุณต้องไป | You must to go. | You **must go**. (no "to") |
| ฉันควรจะเรียน | I should to study. | I **should study**. (no "to") |
| คุณไม่ต้องมา | You mustn't come. | You **don't have to** come. (≠ห้าม) |

> **Must** = ต้อง (obligation). **Don't have to** = ไม่จำเป็น (optional). Don't confuse them!
