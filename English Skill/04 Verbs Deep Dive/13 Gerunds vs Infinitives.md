# Gerunds vs Infinitives

When one verb follows another, the second verb takes either `-ing` (gerund) or `to + V1` (infinitive). There's no one rule — you must learn which verbs take which pattern.

---

## The Two Forms

| Form | Structure | Example | As... |
|------|-----------|---------|-------|
| **Gerund** | V + **Ving** | I enjoy **swimming**. | Noun (activity) |
| **Infinitive** | V + **to V1** | I want **to swim**. | Purpose / intention |

---

## Verbs Followed by Gerund (-ing)

These verbs always take `Ving` after them:

| Verb | Example |
|------|---------|
| **enjoy** | I enjoy **reading**. |
| **avoid** | She avoids **eating** sugar. |
| **consider** | We're considering **moving**. |
| **finish** | He finished **writing** the report. |
| **mind** | Do you mind **waiting**? |
| **suggest** | I suggest **leaving** early. |
| **recommend** | They recommend **booking** in advance. |
| **miss** | I miss **living** in Bangkok. |
| **practice** | You should practice **speaking**. |
| **admit** | He admitted **stealing** the money. |
| **deny** | She denied **knowing** about it. |
| **risk** | Don't risk **losing** everything. |
| **imagine** | Imagine **living** on Mars. |
| **keep** | Keep **trying**! |

### After Prepositions → Always Gerund

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| preposition + Ving | I'm interested **in learning** Thai. |
| | Thank you **for coming**. |
| | She's good **at singing**. |
| | He left **without saying** goodbye. |
| | I'm tired **of waiting**. |

---

## Verbs Followed by Infinitive (to V1)

| Verb | Example |
|------|---------|
| **want** | I want **to go**. |
| **need** | You need **to study**. |
| **decide** | We decided **to leave**. |
| **plan** | She plans **to travel** next year. |
| **hope** | I hope **to see** you soon. |
| **promise** | He promised **to help**. |
| **agree** | They agreed **to pay**. |
| **refuse** | She refused **to answer**. |
| **learn** | I learned **to swim** at age 5. |
| **afford** | I can't afford **to buy** a car. |
| **offer** | He offered **to drive**. |
| **manage** | She managed **to finish** on time. |
| **seem** | You seem **to be** tired. |
| **pretend** | He pretended **to be** asleep. |

---

## Verbs That Can Take Both (Different Meanings!)

| Verb | + Gerund | + Infinitive |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **stop** | Stop **smoking**. (quit the habit) | Stop **to smoke**. (pause what you're doing to have a cigarette) |
| **remember** | I remember **meeting** her. (memory of past event) | Remember **to call** me. (don't forget future action) |
| **forget** | I forgot **sending** the email. (did it, but don't remember) | I forgot **to send** the email. (didn't do it) |
| **try** | Try **restarting** the computer. (experiment) | I tried **to call** but no answer. (attempt, effort) |
| **regret** | I regret **saying** that. (sorry about past action) | I regret **to inform** you... (formal announcement of bad news) |

---

## Gerund as Subject

| ✅ Gerund as Subject | ❌ Infinitive as Subject (archaic/poetic) |
|---------------------|----------------------------------------|
| **Smoking** is bad for you. | To smoke is bad for you. (correct but unnatural) |
| **Learning** English takes time. | To learn English takes time. |
| **Traveling** broadens the mind. | To travel broadens the mind. |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai verbs don't change form — the concept of choosing between -ing and to+verb doesn't exist.

| ❌ | ✅ |
|---|-----|
| I enjoy to read. | I enjoy **reading**. |
| She suggested to go. | She suggested **going**. |
| I want going. | I want **to go**. |
| Stop to smoke. (if you mean quit) | Stop **smoking**. |

> **Quick check:** If it comes after a preposition (in, on, at, for, of, about, without) → always **Ving**.
