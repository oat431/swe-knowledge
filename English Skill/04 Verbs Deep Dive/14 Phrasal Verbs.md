# Phrasal Verbs

A phrasal verb = verb + particle (preposition/adverb) that together create a meaning different from the individual words. They're everywhere in spoken English — and they're a nightmare for learners because you can't guess the meaning from the parts.

---

## Why They Matter

| Normal English | Phrasal Verb | Tone |
|---------------|-------------|------|
| I will investigate the problem. | I'll **look into** it. | Natural, conversational |
| Please continue. | Please **carry on**. | Native-sounding |
| He surrendered. | He **gave up**. | Everyday speech |

> Using phrasal verbs makes you sound like a native speaker. Avoiding them makes you sound like a textbook.

---

## Types of Phrasal Verbs

### 1. Intransitive (No Object)

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| **wake up** | stop sleeping | I **wake up** at 7. |
| **show up** | arrive | He didn't **show up**. |
| **break down** | stop working (machine/car) | My car **broke down**. |
| **grow up** | become an adult | I **grew up** in Bangkok. |
| **pass out** | faint | She **passed out** from the heat. |

### 2. Transitive — Separable (Object Can Go in the Middle)

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| **turn on/off** | start/stop a device | **Turn** the light **on**. / **Turn on** the light. |
| **pick up** | collect, lift | I'll **pick** you **up** at 8. |
| **put off** | postpone | Don't **put** it **off**. |
| **give up** | quit | She **gave** smoking **up**. |
| **take off** | remove (clothing), depart (plane) | **Take** your shoes **off**. |
| **make up** | invent (story), reconcile | He **made** the story **up**. |
| **figure out** | solve, understand | I can't **figure** this **out**. |

> **With pronouns, MUST separate:** "Turn **it** on" ✅ — "Turn on it" ❌

### 3. Transitive — Inseparable (Object Always After)

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| **look after** | take care of | She **looks after** her grandmother. |
| **look for** | search for | I'm **looking for** my keys. |
| **look forward to** | anticipate with pleasure | I **look forward to** meeting you. |
| **run into** | meet by chance | I **ran into** an old friend. |
| **get over** | recover from | It took weeks to **get over** the flu. |
| **go through** | experience (difficulty) | She's **going through** a divorce. |
| **come across** | find by chance | I **came across** an interesting article. |

### 4. Three-Part Phrasal Verbs (Always Inseparable)

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| **put up with** | tolerate | I can't **put up with** the noise. |
| **get along with** | have good relationship | Do you **get along with** your boss? |
| **come up with** | think of (idea/solution) | She **came up with** a great plan. |
| **look up to** | admire | I've always **looked up to** my father. |
| **cut down on** | reduce | I need to **cut down on** sugar. |
| **drop out of** | quit (school, course) | He **dropped out of** university. |

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai doesn't have phrasal verbs — this is a concept you have to build from scratch.

| ❌ | ✅ |
|---|-----|
| I search my key. | I'm **looking for** my key. |
| Please continue your work. (textbook) | Please **carry on** with your work. (natural) |
| I can't tolerate the noise. | I can't **put up with** the noise. |
| She cares her mother. | She **looks after** her mother. |

> **Pro tip:** Don't memorize lists. Learn phrasal verbs in **context** — one at a time, in a real sentence. Your brain remembers stories, not lists.
