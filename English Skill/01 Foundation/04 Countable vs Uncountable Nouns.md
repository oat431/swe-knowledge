# Countable vs Uncountable Nouns

English divides nouns into things you can count and things you can't. This affects which articles, quantifiers, and verb forms you use.

---

## The Two Types

| | Countable | Uncountable |
|---|-----------|-------------|
| **Can you...** | Count them? (1 apple, 2 apples) | Count them? ❌ (water, not "2 waters") |
| **Articles** | a/an, the | the, (no article) |
| **Plural?** | Yes: apples, books, cars | No plural form: water, rice, information |
| **Quantifiers** | many, few, several | much, little, a bit of |

---

## Common Uncountable Nouns

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Liquids** | water, milk, coffee, oil, juice |
| **Gases** | air, oxygen, smoke, steam |
| **Solids (materials)** | wood, metal, plastic, paper, glass |
| **Abstract ideas** | love, happiness, information, advice, knowledge |
| **Subjects** | mathematics, physics, economics |
| **Food (bulk)** | rice, sugar, flour, bread, meat |
| **Natural phenomena** | rain, snow, thunder, lightning, weather |
| **Languages** | English, Thai, Chinese |

---

## Quantifiers: Which to Use

| Quantifier | Countable | Uncountable | Example |
|-----------|:---------:|:-----------:|---------|
| many | ✅ | ❌ | many books |
| much | ❌ | ✅ | much water |
| a lot of / lots of | ✅ | ✅ | a lot of books / a lot of water |
| few / a few | ✅ | ❌ | a few problems |
| little / a little | ❌ | ✅ | a little time |
| some | ✅ | ✅ | some books / some water |
| any | ✅ | ✅ | any books? / any water? |
| several | ✅ | ❌ | several ideas |
| plenty of | ✅ | ✅ | plenty of time / plenty of books |

### Few vs A Few, Little vs A Little

| Expression | Meaning | Tone |
|-----------|---------|------|
| **a few** / **a little** | Some (enough) | Positive 🙂 |
| **few** / **little** | Not enough (nearly none) | Negative 😟 |

> "I have **a few** friends." (some — I'm okay)
> "I have **few** friends." (almost none — I'm lonely)

---

## Nouns That Can Be Both

Some nouns change meaning depending on countable vs uncountable use:

| Noun | Uncountable Meaning | Countable Meaning |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **paper** | Material | A newspaper / academic paper |
| **glass** | Material | A drinking glass |
| **hair** | All the hair on your head | A single strand |
| **time** | Duration (minutes, hours) | An occasion (three times) |
| **work** | Job, labor | A work of art |
| **experience** | Knowledge gained | An event (an experience) |
| **chicken** | Food (meat) | The animal |

> "I ate **chicken**." (uncountable — the meat)
> "I saw **a chicken**." (countable — the animal)

---

## Making Uncountable Nouns Countable

Use "unit" words:

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| a **piece** of... | a piece of advice, a piece of furniture |
| a **glass** of... | a glass of water, a glass of milk |
| a **cup** of... | a cup of coffee, a cup of tea |
| a **slice** of... | a slice of bread, a slice of cake |
| a **bit** of... | a bit of information, a bit of luck |
| a **sheet** of... | a sheet of paper |
| a **bar** of... | a bar of chocolate, a bar of soap |

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai doesn't distinguish countable/uncountable — classifiers (ลักษณะนาม) work differently.

| Thai Pattern | ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|-------------|---------|-----------|
| ขอน้ำสอง | Can I have two waters? | Can I have two **glasses of** water? |
| ฉันมีข้อมูล | I have many informations. | I have a lot of **information**. |
| เฟอร์นิเจอร์สามชิ้น | three furnitures | three **pieces of** furniture |

---

## Quick Test

1. How ___ (many/much) money do you have? → **much**
2. I have ___ (a few/a little) ideas. → **a few**
3. She gave me good ___ (advice/advices). → **advice**
4. There aren't ___ (many/much) chairs. → **many**
5. I need ___ (a few/a little) help. → **a little**
