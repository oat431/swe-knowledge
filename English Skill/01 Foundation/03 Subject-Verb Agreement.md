# Subject-Verb Agreement

In English, the subject and verb must match in number. Singular subjects take singular verbs; plural subjects take plural verbs. It sounds simple — but there are many traps.

---

## The Basic Rule

| Subject | Verb Form | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Singular (he/she/it) | Verb + s/es | He **runs** every day. |
| Plural (they/we) | Base verb | They **run** every day. |
| I | Base verb (exception) | I **run** every day. |
| You (singular or plural) | Base verb | You **run** every day. |

---

## Tricky Cases

### 1. Words Between Subject and Verb

The verb agrees with the **subject**, not the nearest noun.

| ✅ Correct | ❌ Wrong |
|-----------|---------|
| The **box** of chocolates **is** expensive. | The box of chocolates **are** expensive. |
| The **students** in the class **are** tired. | The students in the class **is** tired. |

### 2. Either/Or and Neither/Nor

The verb agrees with the **closest** subject.

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Either A **or** B → verb agrees with B | Either the **teacher** or the **students are** responsible. |
| Neither A **nor** B → verb agrees with B | Neither the **students** nor the **teacher is** responsible. |

### 3. Indefinite Pronouns

| Always Singular | Always Plural | Depends |
|----------------|---------------|---------|
| everyone, everyone, someone, anyone | both, few, many, several | all, some, none, most |
| each, every, either, neither | | |
| nobody, nothing, no one | | |
| **Everybody loves** pizza. ✅ | **Both are** correct. ✅ | **Some of the cake is** gone. / **Some of the cookies are** gone. |

### 4. Collective Nouns

| Treat as Singular (one unit) | Treat as Plural (individual members) |
|------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| The **team is** winning. | The **team are** arguing among themselves. |
| The **family is** large. | The **family are** all going their separate ways. |

> American English prefers singular; British English uses both.

### 5. There is / There are

The subject comes **after** the verb.

| ✅ Correct | ❌ Wrong |
|-----------|---------|
| There **is** a **book** on the table. | There are a book on the table. |
| There **are** three **books** on the table. | There is three books on the table. |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai verbs don't change form based on subject — this concept doesn't exist in Thai.

| Thai Pattern | ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|-------------|---------|-----------|
| เขาไปโรงเรียน | He go to school. | He **goes** to school. |
| ฉันชอบหมา | I likes dogs. | I **like** dogs. (no -s for "I") |

---

## Quick Test

1. Everyone ___ (is/are) welcome. → **is**
2. The list of items ___ (is/are) long. → **is**
3. Neither the cat nor the dogs ___ (was/were) fed. → **were**
4. There ___ (is/are) many problems. → **are**
5. The team ___ (has/have) decided to play their best. → **has** (American) / **have** (British)
