# Parts of Speech

Every English word belongs to one of eight categories. Understanding these is the first step to building correct sentences.

---

## The Eight Parts

| # | Part | Job | Examples | Thai |
|---|------|-----|----------|------|
| 1 | **Noun** | Names a person, place, thing, or idea | `dog`, `Bangkok`, `freedom`, `computer` | คำนาม |
| 2 | **Pronoun** | Replaces a noun | `I`, `you`, `he`, `she`, `it`, `we`, `they` | สรรพนาม |
| 3 | **Verb** | Shows action or state | `run`, `eat`, `is`, `become`, `think` | คำกริยา |
| 4 | **Adjective** | Describes a noun | `big`, `beautiful`, `red`, `happy` | คำคุณศัพท์ |
| 5 | **Adverb** | Describes a verb, adjective, or adverb | `quickly`, `very`, `yesterday`, `always` | คำกริยาวิเศษณ์ |
| 6 | **Preposition** | Shows relationship (position, time, direction) | `in`, `on`, `at`, `from`, `to`, `with` | คำบุพบท |
| 7 | **Conjunction** | Connects words, phrases, or clauses | `and`, `but`, `or`, `because`, `although` | คำสันธาน |
| 8 | **Interjection** | Expresses emotion | `Wow!`, `Oh!`, `Ouch!`, `Hey!` | คำอุทาน |

---

## 1. Nouns

| Type | Example | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| Common | `city`, `book` | General things |
| Proper | `London`, `Harry Potter` | Specific names — always capitalized |
| Abstract | `love`, `happiness` | Ideas, feelings — can't touch |
| Concrete | `table`, `water` | Physical things — can touch |
| Collective | `team`, `family` | Group treated as one unit |

## 2. Pronouns

| Type | Examples | Replaces... |
|------|----------|------------|
| Subject | `I`, `you`, `he`, `she`, `it`, `we`, `they` | Who does the action |
| Object | `me`, `you`, `him`, `her`, `it`, `us`, `them` | Who receives the action |
| Possessive | `my`, `your`, `his`, `her`, `its`, `our`, `their` | Who owns something |
| Reflexive | `myself`, `yourself`, `himself` | Subject = object ("I did it myself") |
| Demonstrative | `this`, `that`, `these`, `those` | Pointing to something |

## 3. Verbs

| Type | Example | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| Action | `run`, `write`, `build` | Physical or mental action |
| Linking | `is`, `seems`, `becomes` | Connects subject to description |
| Helping (Auxiliary) | `is`, `have`, `do`, `will`, `can` | Helps main verb form tense/mood |
| Transitive | `She ate **an apple**` | Needs an object |
| Intransitive | `She **sleeps**` | No object needed |

## 4. Adjectives

Describe nouns. Answer: *Which one? What kind? How many?*

- **Order matters in English:** Opinion → Size → Age → Shape → Color → Origin → Material → Noun
- "A **beautiful small old round red Italian wooden** table" (natural)
- "A wooden Italian red old round small beautiful table" (sounds wrong)

## 5. Adverbs

Describe verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. Often end in `-ly`.

| Type | Example | Answers |
|------|---------|---------|
| Manner | `quickly`, `carefully` | How? |
| Time | `yesterday`, `soon` | When? |
| Place | `here`, `everywhere` | Where? |
| Frequency | `always`, `sometimes`, `never` | How often? |
| Degree | `very`, `extremely`, `quite` | To what extent? |

## 6. Prepositions

Show relationships in **time** (`at 5pm`, `on Monday`, `in June`), **place** (`at the door`, `on the table`, `in the room`), and **direction** (`to school`, `from Bangkok`).

## 7. Conjunctions

See [[Conjunctions]] for full detail. Three types: coordinating (FANBOYS: `for, and, nor, but, or, yet, so`), subordinating (`because`, `although`, `if`), and correlative (`either...or`, `neither...nor`).

## 8. Interjections

Standalone words expressing emotion: `Wow!`, `Oh no!`, `Oops!`, `Yay!`, `Hmm...`

## Quick Test

Identify the part of speech for each bold word:

> The **happy** dog **quickly** ran **to** the park **and** played **with** his ball.

| Word | Part |
|------|------|
| happy | Adjective |
| quickly | Adverb |
| to | Preposition |
| and | Conjunction |
| with | Preposition |
