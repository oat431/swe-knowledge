# Articles: A, An, The

Articles are tiny words that cause huge problems — especially for Thai speakers (Thai has no articles). The choice between `a/an`, `the`, and no article depends on whether you're talking about something specific or general.

---

## The Three Choices

| Article | Use When... | Example |
|---------|------------|---------|
| **a / an** | Any one of a group (non-specific, first mention) | "I saw **a** dog." (any dog) |
| **the** | A specific one (both speaker and listener know which) | "**The** dog was brown." (that specific dog) |
| **(no article)** | General meaning, plural, uncountable | "**Dogs** are loyal." (all dogs) |

---

## A vs An — The Sound Rule

Use **a** before consonant **sounds**, **an** before vowel **sounds**.

| ✅ Correct | ❌ Wrong | Why |
|-----------|---------|-----|
| **a** university | an university | "university" starts with /j/ (y-sound) |
| **an** hour | a hour | "hour" starts with /aʊ/ (h is silent) |
| **an** MBA | a MBA | "M" is pronounced /em/ (vowel sound) |
| **a** European | an European | "European" starts with /j/ |

> **Rule:** It's the **sound**, not the spelling.

---

## Definite Article: "The"

Use `the` when:

| Situation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Second mention** (already introduced) | "I saw a dog. **The** dog was big." |
| **Unique things** (only one exists) | "**The** sun", "**The** internet", "**The** president" |
| **Shared knowledge** (both people know which) | "Close **the** door." (the door in this room) |
| **Superlatives** | "**The** best", "**The** tallest" |
| **Ordinal numbers** | "**The** first", "**The** second" |
| **Specified by phrase/clause** | "**The** man **in the blue shirt**" |

---

## No Article (Zero Article)

Omit articles when:

| Situation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **General plural** | "Cats are independent." (all cats, generally) |
| **Uncountable nouns (general)** | "Water is essential." (water in general) |
| **Proper nouns** | "I live in Thailand." (but: "**the** United States") |
| **Meals** | "Let's have breakfast." (but: "**The** breakfast was delicious") |
| **Languages / Subjects** | "She studies English." |
| **Sports / Games** | "I play football." |

---

## Common Patterns

| Pattern | Use | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|
| `There is a/an...` | Introducing something new | "There is **a** problem." |
| `It's the...` | Identifying a specific one | "It's **the** problem I mentioned." |
| `...of the...` | Almost always `the` | "**The** back of **the** room" |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| Mistake | Why It Happens | Fix |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| "I bought car." ❌ | Thai doesn't use articles | → "I bought **a** car." |
| "She is teacher." ❌ | Thai: เธอเป็นครู (no article) | → "She is **a** teacher." |
| "Sun is hot." ❌ | Unique things need "the" | → "**The** sun is hot." |
| "I go to the school." (when meaning generally) | Over-correction | → "I go to school." |

---

## Quick Test

Fill in `a`, `an`, `the`, or `—` (no article):

1. I need ___ new computer. → **a**
2. She is ___ best student in class. → **the**
3. ___ water in this glass is cold. → **The**
4. He is ___ honest man. → **an** (silent h)
5. I love ___ dogs. → **—** (general plural)
