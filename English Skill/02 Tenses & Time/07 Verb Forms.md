# Verb Forms: V1, V2, V3, Ving

Every English verb has up to 5 forms. Understanding these is the foundation for all tense construction.

---

## The Five Verb Forms

| # | Form | Name | Use | Example (go) | Example (eat) |
|---|------|------|-----|-------------|--------------|
| V1 | Base | Infinitive | Present Simple (except he/she/it), modals, imperatives | go | eat |
| V1+s | Third Person Singular | Present Simple (he/she/it) | goes | eats |
| V2 | Past | Past Simple | Completed past actions | went | ate |
| V3 | Past Participle | Perfect tenses, Passive | has gone, was eaten | gone | eaten |
| Ving | Present Participle / Gerund | Continuous tenses, gerunds | am going, going is fun | eating |

---

## Regular vs Irregular Verbs

### Regular Verbs (Predictable Pattern)

| V1 | V2 | V3 | Rule |
|----|----|-----|------|
| work | work**ed** | work**ed** | +ed |
| live | liv**ed** | liv**ed** | +d (ends in e) |
| study | stud**ied** | stud**ied** | y → ied |
| stop | stop**ped** | stop**ped** | double consonant + ed |

## Which Form Goes Where

| Construction | Form | Example |
|-------------|------|---------|
| Present Simple (I/you/we/they) | V1 | I **work** here. |
| Present Simple (he/she/it) | V1+s | She **works** here. |
| Past Simple | V2 | I **worked** yesterday. |
| After modals (can, will, must) | V1 | I can **swim**. |
| Present Perfect | have/has + **V3** | I have **finished**. |
| Past Perfect | had + **V3** | I had **finished**. |
| Passive voice | be + **V3** | It was **made** in China. |
| Continuous tenses | be + **Ving** | I am **working**. |
| After prepositions | **Ving** | Thank you for **coming**. |
| As a noun (gerund) | **Ving** | **Smoking** is bad. |

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai verbs never change form. This is the #1 habit to break.

| Thai Pattern | ❌ | ✅ |
|-------------|---|---|
| เมื่อวานฉันไป | Yesterday I **go**. | Yesterday I **went**. |
| ฉันไปมาแล้ว | I **go** already. | I **have gone** already. |
| มันทำในไทย | It **make** in Thailand. | It **is made** in Thailand. |

> **Memory trick:** Learn irregular verbs in groups by sound pattern: sing-sang-sung, ring-rang-rung, swim-swam-swum. Your brain remembers patterns better than isolated words.
