# Active vs Passive Voice

Voice tells us whether the subject **does** the action or **receives** it. Choosing the right voice makes your English sound natural — and wrong voice is a dead giveaway of a non-native speaker.

---

## The Two Voices

| Voice | Focus | Structure | Example |
|-------|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Active** | Who DOES the action | S + V + O | The chef **cooked** the meal. |
| **Passive** | Who RECEIVES the action | S + be + V3 (+ by agent) | The meal **was cooked** (by the chef). |

> **Active:** Subject → acts. **Passive:** Subject ← acted upon.

---

## Passive Voice Formula

| Tense | Active | Passive |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Present Simple | She **writes** books. | Books **are written** (by her). |
| Past Simple | She **wrote** a book. | A book **was written** (by her). |
| Present Continuous | She **is writing** a book. | A book **is being written**. |
| Past Continuous | She **was writing** a book. | A book **was being written**. |
| Present Perfect | She **has written** a book. | A book **has been written**. |
| Past Perfect | She **had written** a book. | A book **had been written**. |
| Future Simple | She **will write** a book. | A book **will be written**. |
| Modals | She **can write** a book. | A book **can be written**. |

**The pattern:** `be + V3` — just conjugate `be` to match the tense.

---

## When to Use Passive Voice

| Situation | Active (weaker) | Passive (better) |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Agent is unknown** | Someone stole my bike. | My bike **was stolen**. ✅ |
| **Agent is obvious/unimportant** | Workers built this house in 1920. | This house **was built** in 1920. ✅ |
| **Focus on the result** | The committee approved the proposal. | The proposal **was approved**. ✅ |
| **Formal / academic writing** | We conducted the experiment. | The experiment **was conducted**. ✅ |
| **Avoiding blame** | I made a mistake. | A mistake **was made**. (⚠️ often weaselly) |

---

## When NOT to Use Passive

| ❌ Over-passive | ✅ Active (better) |
|----------------|-------------------|
| The ball was hit by him. | He hit the ball. |
| Dinner was eaten by me. | I ate dinner. |
| A good time was had by all. | Everyone had a good time. |

> **Rule of thumb:** Active is clearer and stronger. Use passive only when you have a reason.

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai uses `ถูก` (thùuk) for passive — but only for negative/unwanted events. English passive has no such restriction.

| Thai Mindset | ❌ Avoided (unnatural in English) | ✅ Natural English |
|-------------|----------------------------------|-------------------|
| ถูก = bad only | (avoiding passive for positive events) | "She **was promoted** last week." |
| No passive habit | The police caught the thief. | "The thief **was caught**." (focus on thief) |

---

## Quick Test

Convert to passive:

1. They built this bridge in 1950. → This bridge **was built** in 1950.
2. Someone has stolen my phone. → My phone **has been stolen**.
3. They will announce the results tomorrow. → The results **will be announced** tomorrow.
4. People speak English worldwide. → English **is spoken** worldwide.
