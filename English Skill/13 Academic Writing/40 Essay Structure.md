# Essay Structure

IELTS Task 2 and TOEFL Independent Writing both require a well-organized essay. A clear structure is the difference between Band 6 and Band 7+ (IELTS) or Fair and Good (TOEFL).

---

## The Standard 4-Paragraph Essay

| Section | Purpose | Length (IELTS 250+ words) | Length (TOEFL 300+ words) |
|---------|---------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Introduction** | State the topic + your position | 2-3 sentences (~40 words) | 3-4 sentences (~50 words) |
| **Body 1** | First main argument | 4-5 sentences (~80 words) | 5-6 sentences (~100 words) |
| **Body 2** | Second main argument | 4-5 sentences (~80 words) | 5-6 sentences (~100 words) |
| **Conclusion** | Summarize + restate position | 2-3 sentences (~40 words) | 3-4 sentences (~50 words) |

> Some students prefer 3 body paragraphs. That's fine too — but 2 is safer for time management.

---

## The Introduction

### Formula

```
Hook/Background + Paraphrase the Question + Thesis Statement
```

### Structure

| Element | What It Does | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Hook** | Introduce the topic broadly | "Technology has transformed the way people communicate." |
| **Paraphrase** | Restate the question in your own words | "Some believe this has improved relationships, while others argue it has made them weaker." |
| **Thesis** | State your position clearly | "In my opinion, the benefits outweigh the drawbacks." |

### Example Introduction

> **Question:** Some people think that social media has a negative effect on society. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

> "Social media platforms have become an integral part of modern life, connecting billions of people worldwide. While some argue that these platforms have a detrimental impact on society, I believe that the advantages, such as improved communication and access to information, far outweigh the disadvantages."

### ✅ Good Thesis Statements

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Agree** | "I strongly agree that..." |
| **Disagree** | "I disagree with this view because..." |
| **Partially agree** | "While I agree that X, I believe Y..." |
| **Both sides + opinion** | "There are valid arguments on both sides, but I believe..." |

### ❌ Avoid These

| ❌ Weak | Why |
|---------|-----|
| "This essay will discuss..." | Too mechanical — IELTS examiners don't like this |
| "In this essay I will talk about..." | Same — too formulaic |
| "Nowadays..." | Overused — try "In recent years..." or "Currently..." |

## Body Paragraphs

### Formula (PEEL)

```
Point → Explain → Example → Link
```

| Element | What It Does | Signal Words |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **P**oint | State your argument | "Firstly," "The main reason is..." |
| **E**xplain | Why this is true | "This is because..." |
| **E**xample | Specific evidence | "For instance," "A good example is..." |
| **L**ink | Connect back to thesis | "Therefore," "This demonstrates that..." |

### Example Body Paragraph

> "Firstly, social media allows people to maintain relationships across long distances. **(Point)** This is particularly important for families who live in different countries, as they can stay connected through video calls and instant messaging. **(Explain)** For example, my cousin who studies in Australia uses LINE to talk to our family in Thailand every day, which helps them feel close despite the distance. **(Example)** Therefore, rather than weakening relationships, social media can actually strengthen them. **(Link)"**

## The Conclusion

### Formula

```
Summary + Restate Position + Final Thought (optional)
```

| Element | What It Does | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Summary** | Briefly restate main points | "In conclusion, while there are some drawbacks..." |
| **Restate position** | Remind the reader of your view | "I believe the benefits are more significant." |
| **Final thought** | Broader implication (optional) | "If used responsibly, social media can be a powerful tool for connection." |

### Example Conclusion

> "In conclusion, although social media can have negative effects such as addiction and misinformation, I believe its ability to connect people and share knowledge makes it a positive force in society. If individuals use these platforms responsibly, the benefits will continue to outweigh the drawbacks."

### ❌ Avoid These in Conclusions

| ❌ Weak | Why |
|---------|-----|
| "To sum up..." | Too informal for academic essays |
| Introducing NEW ideas | The conclusion summarizes — it doesn't add |
| "In a nutshell..." | Too casual |

## Essay Planning (5 Minutes)

Before you start writing, spend 5 minutes planning:

| Step | Time | What to Do |
|------|------|-----------|
| 1 | 1 min | Understand the question — what exactly is being asked? |
| 2 | 1 min | Decide your position — agree, disagree, or partial? |
| 3 | 1 min | Brainstorm 2-3 main arguments |
| 4 | 1 min | Think of examples for each argument |
| 5 | 1 min | Outline your 4 paragraphs |

> **5 minutes of planning saves 15 minutes of writing.** Don't skip this.

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| Trap | Problem | Fix |
|------|---------|-----|
| No clear thesis | "I think maybe social media is OK I guess." | State your position directly: "I believe social media is beneficial." |
| Only one body paragraph | Not enough development | Always write at least 2 body paragraphs |
| Listing without explaining | "It's good because A, B, C, D." | Pick 2 strong points and develop them fully |
| Conclusion introduces new ideas | Confusing the reader | Only summarize — don't add new arguments |
| No examples | Arguments feel weak | Every body paragraph needs one specific example |
