# IELTS Task 1 — Describing Data

IELTS Writing Task 1 gives you a **graph, chart, table, diagram, or map** and asks you to describe it in 150+ words (20 minutes). This is NOT an essay — it's a **report**.

---

## Task Types

| Type | What You See | Key Language |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Line graph** | Trends over time | "increased," "decreased," "fluctuated" |
| **Bar chart** | Comparisons | "higher than," "lower than," "the most" |
| **Pie chart** | Proportions/percentages | "accounts for," "represents," "the majority" |
| **Table** | Raw data | "the highest," "the lowest," "approximately" |
| **Process diagram** | Steps in a process | "first," "then," "following this," "finally" |
| **Map** | Location changes over time | "was replaced by," "was built," "was demolished" |

---

## Structure (4 Paragraphs)

| Paragraph | Content | Length |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| **1. Introduction** | Paraphrase the question | 1-2 sentences |
| **2. Overview** | 2-3 main trends/patterns (no data) | 2-3 sentences |
| **3. Detail A** | Specific data for first group/trend | 3-4 sentences |
| **4. Detail B** | Specific data for second group/trend | 3-4 sentences |

---

## Paragraph 1: Introduction

**Rule:** Paraphrase the question. Never copy it word for word.

| Original | Paraphrased |
|----------|------------|
| "The graph **shows**..." | "The graph **illustrates / depicts / presents**..." |
| "**the number of** tourists" | "**the figure for** tourists / **visitor numbers**" |
| "**from** 2010 **to** 2020" | "**between** 2010 **and** 2020 / **over a 10-year period**" |
| "**in** Thailand" | "**in** Thailand / **across** Thailand" |

### Example

> **Question:** The bar chart shows the percentage of households with internet access in four countries in 2020.

> **Introduction:** "The bar chart illustrates the proportion of households that had internet access in four different countries in 2020."

---

## Paragraph 2: Overview

**The most important paragraph.** Without an overview, you cannot score above Band 5 for Task Achievement.

**What to include:** The 2-3 most noticeable trends or patterns — NO specific numbers.

| Signal | Example |
|--------|---------|
| "Overall,..." | "Overall, Country A had the highest rate of internet access." |
| "It is clear that..." | "It is clear that internet usage increased across all countries." |
| "The most striking feature is..." | "The most striking feature is the gap between developed and developing nations." |

### Example

> "Overall, internet access was highest in developed countries, with Japan and the UK leading. In contrast, India and Nigeria had significantly lower rates. All four countries showed an increase over the period."

---

## Paragraphs 3-4: Detailed Description

Group your data logically. Don't describe every number — select key data points.

### Grouping Strategies

| Strategy | When to Use |
|----------|------------|
| **Highest + Lowest** | When there's a clear gap |
| **Increasing + Decreasing** | When trends go in different directions |
| **Similar groups** | When some items are close together |
| **Time periods** | Early period vs. later period |

### Useful Language for Data

#### Describing Trends

| Trend | Verb | Noun |
|-------|------|------|
| Up | increase, rise, grow, climb, surge | an increase, a rise, a growth |
| Down | decrease, fall, drop, decline, plummet | a decrease, a fall, a decline |
| Stable | remain stable, stay the same, level off | stability, no change |
| Fluctuate | fluctuate, vary, go up and down | fluctuation, variation |
| Peak | peak, reach a peak, reach a high point | a peak, a high point |
| Bottom | bottom out, reach a low point | a low point, a trough |

#### Describing Speed

| Speed | Adverbs |
|-------|---------|
| Fast | rapidly, sharply, dramatically, significantly, steeply |
| Medium | steadily, moderately, gradually |
| Slow | slightly, marginally, minimally |

#### Describing Amounts

| Precision | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Exact** | "50%," "3.2 million" |
| **Approximate** | "approximately 50%," "around 3 million," "just over half" |
| **Comparative** | "twice as high," "three times more," "half of" |
| **Superlative** | "the highest," "the lowest," "the most significant" |

### Example Detail Paragraph

> "Japan had the highest internet access rate, at approximately 93%, followed by the UK at 87%. In contrast, India had significantly lower access, with only 34% of households connected. Nigeria had the lowest figure, at just 18%. Notably, the gap between the highest and lowest countries was over 75 percentage points."

## Process Diagrams & Maps

### Process Diagram

| Structure | Language |
|-----------|---------|
| Step 1 | "First," "The process begins with..." |
| Step 2 | "Next," "Following this," "Subsequently" |
| Step 3 | "Then," "After that," "The next stage involves..." |
| Final | "Finally," "In the final stage," "The process concludes when..." |

### Maps

| Change | Language |
|--------|---------|
| New building | "A new hospital was built/constructed in..." |
| Demolished | "The old factory was demolished/knocked down." |
| Replaced | "The park was replaced by a shopping centre." |
| Expanded | "The school was expanded to include..." |
| Renamed | "The street was renamed Main Street." |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| Trap | Problem | Fix |
|------|---------|-----|
| No overview | Band 5 ceiling for Task Achievement | Always write 2-3 overview sentences |
| Copying the question | Penalized for not paraphrasing | Change at least 3-4 words |
| Describing every number | Too detailed, no clear pattern | Select key data points — highest, lowest, biggest change |
| No comparisons | Just listing numbers | Always compare: "higher than," "double," "in contrast" |
| Using "I" or giving opinions | Task 1 is a report, not an essay | Never say "I think" or "In my opinion" |
