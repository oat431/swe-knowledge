# Coherence & Cohesion

Coherence = your essay makes **logical sense**. Cohesion = your sentences are **connected smoothly**. Together, they're 25% of your IELTS Writing score and a major factor in TOEFL scoring.

---

## Coherence (Logical Flow)

Your ideas must follow a logical order. The reader should never think "why is this here?"

### Paragraph-Level Coherence

| Rule | ✅ Good | ❌ Bad |
|------|---------|--------|
| **One idea per paragraph** | Body 1 = only about education | Body 1 = education + health + environment |
| **Logical order** | Introduction → Body 1 → Body 2 → Conclusion | Jumping between ideas randomly |
| **Clear topic sentence** | "The main benefit of exercise is improved health." | (no topic sentence — reader confused) |
| **Consistent focus** | Paragraph stays on topic | Paragraph drifts to unrelated ideas |

### Topic Sentences

Every body paragraph should start with a **topic sentence** that tells the reader what the paragraph is about.

| ❌ Weak | ✅ Strong |
|---------|----------|
| "There are many things about education." | "The most significant benefit of online education is its accessibility." |
| "Another point is about health." | "Regular exercise also has a profound impact on mental health." |
| "Now about the environment." | "In addition to health benefits, cycling reduces carbon emissions." |

## Cohesion (Smooth Connection)

Cohesion connects sentences and paragraphs using **linking devices**.

### Sentence-Level Cohesion

| Device | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **Pronouns** | "Social media has benefits. **It** connects people." |
| **Synonyms** | "Exercise is important. Physical **activity** improves health." |
| **Repetition** | "The policy failed. This **policy** was poorly designed." |
| **Linking words** | "Exercise is good. **Moreover**, it reduces stress." |

### Paragraph-Level Cohesion (Transitions)

| Transition | Function | Example |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Firstly / First** | Starting body 1 | "Firstly, education improves employment prospects." |
| **Secondly / Second** | Starting body 2 | "Secondly, education promotes critical thinking." |
| **In addition / Moreover** | Adding a similar point | "Moreover, education reduces poverty." |
| **However / Nevertheless** | Contrasting | "However, education alone is not enough." |
| **For example / For instance** | Giving an example | "For instance, graduates earn 50% more on average." |
| **As a result / Therefore** | Showing consequence | "As a result, governments invest heavily in education." |
| **In conclusion / To summarize** | Starting conclusion | "In conclusion, education is the foundation of development." |

## Linking Word Categories

### Addition

| Formal | Neutral | Informal |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Furthermore | Also | And |
| Moreover | In addition | Plus |
| Additionally | What's more | Besides |

### Contrast

| Formal | Neutral | Informal |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Nevertheless | However | But |
| Nonetheless | On the other hand | Yet |
| Conversely | In contrast | Still |

### Cause & Effect

| Formal | Neutral | Informal |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Consequently | As a result | So |
| Hence | Therefore | That's why |
| Thus | Because of this | Because |

### Examples

| Formal | Neutral | Informal |
|--------|---------|----------|
| To illustrate | For example | Like |
| A case in point is | For instance | Such as |

## Over-Linking (Don't Do This)

| ❌ Over-linked | ✅ Better |
|---------------|----------|
| "**Firstly**, I think exercise is good. **Secondly**, it helps health. **Thirdly**, it reduces stress. **Fourthly**, it improves sleep. **In conclusion**, exercise is great." | "Exercise offers numerous benefits. It improves physical health, reduces stress, and promotes better sleep. **Therefore**, incorporating regular exercise into daily life is highly beneficial." |

> **Rule:** Not every sentence needs a linking word. Use them to connect **ideas**, not every single sentence.

---

## Referencing (Avoiding Repetition)

| Technique | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Pronouns** | "The government introduced a policy. **It** was effective." |
| **Demonstratives** | "Education is important. **This** is why governments invest in it." |
| **Synonyms** | "Students... learners... pupils... young people..." |
| **General terms** | "Carbon emissions... pollution... environmental damage..." |
| **Such/These/This** | "Crime rates increased. **This** trend worried residents." |

## Common Cohesion Errors

| Error | ❌ Example | ✅ Fix |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| **No connection** | "Exercise is good. Education is important." | "Exercise is good for health. **Similarly**, education is important for career success." |
| **Wrong connector** | "I was tired. **Moreover**, I went to bed." | "I was tired. **Therefore**, I went to bed." (cause → effect, not addition) |
| **Comma splice** | "I was tired, I went to bed." | "I was tired, **so** I went to bed." or "I was tired. I went to bed." |
| **Referencing unclear** | "The company and the government discussed it. They agreed." | "The company and the government discussed the issue. **Both parties** agreed." |
| **Repeating the same word** | "Education is important. Education helps people. Education reduces poverty." | "Education is important. **It** helps people and reduces poverty." |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| Trap | Problem | Fix |
|------|---------|-----|
| Using "and" for everything | "And this is good. And also it helps." | Use varied connectors: "Moreover," "Furthermore," "In addition" |
| No topic sentences | Paragraphs start with details | Always start with a clear main idea |
| Repeating the same word | "Education... education... education..." | Use pronouns and synonyms |
| Wrong connector | "I was hungry. **However**, I ate." | "However" = contrast. "Therefore" = result. Choose correctly. |
| Missing transitions between paragraphs | Paragraphs feel disconnected | Start each body paragraph with a transition word |
