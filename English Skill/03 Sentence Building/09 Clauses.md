# Clauses

A clause is a group of words with a subject and a verb. Understanding clauses is the key to building complex, accurate sentences.

---

## Two Types of Clauses

| Type | Can stand alone? | Example |
|------|:---------------:|---------|
| **Independent** (Main) | ✅ Yes | I went home. |
| **Dependent** (Subordinate) | ❌ No | ...because I was tired. |

A dependent clause always needs an independent clause to complete it.

---

## Types of Dependent Clauses

### 1. Relative Clauses (Adjective Clauses)

Describe a noun. Start with: `who`, `whom`, `whose`, `which`, `that`, `where`, `when`.

| Pronoun | Refers to | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **who** | People (subject) | The woman **who** lives next door is a doctor. |
| **whom** | People (object) | The man **whom** I met was kind. (formal) |
| **whose** | Possession | The student **whose** phone rang got in trouble. |
| **which** | Things | The book **which** I bought is boring. |
| **that** | People or things (restrictive) | The car **that** crashed was red. |
| **where** | Places | The cafe **where** we met is closed. |
| **when** | Times | I remember the day **when** we first met. |

### Defining vs Non-Defining

| Type | Meaning | Punctuation | Example |
|------|---------|------------|---------|
| **Defining** (Restrictive) | Essential — identifies WHICH one | No commas | Students **who study** pass. (Only those who study) |
| **Non-defining** | Extra info — already identified | Commas | My mother, **who is 60**, still works. (I have one mother) |

> "My brother who lives in London is a doctor." (I have multiple brothers — this identifies which one)
> "My brother, who lives in London, is a doctor." (I have one brother — extra info about him)

### 2. Noun Clauses

Function as a noun in the sentence. Start with: `that`, `what`, `who`, `where`, `when`, `why`, `how`, `whether`, `if`.

| Role | Example |
|------|---------|
| Subject | **What she said** surprised everyone. |
| Object | I don't know **where he went**. |
| Complement | The problem is **that we have no time**. |

### 3. Adverbial Clauses

Function as an adverb — describe time, reason, condition, contrast, purpose. See [[Sentence Structure]] for full list of subordinating conjunctions.

| Type | Conjunction | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| Time | when, while, after, before | **After** I graduated, I got a job. |
| Reason | because, since | I left **because** I was bored. |
| Condition | if, unless | **If** you study, you'll pass. |
| Contrast | although, even though | **Although** it rained, we went out. |
| Purpose | so that | I wrote it down **so that** I wouldn't forget. |

---

## ⚠️ Common Errors

| Error | ❌ | ✅ |
|-------|---|---|
| Missing relative pronoun | The man ~~(who)~~ I saw him was tall. | The man **who** I saw was tall. |
| Double subject | The book **which I bought it** is good. | The book **which I bought** is good. |
| "That" for people (formal contexts) | The person **that** called... (casual OK) | The person **who** called... (formal) |
