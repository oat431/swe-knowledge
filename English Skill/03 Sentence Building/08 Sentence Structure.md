# Sentence Structure

English sentences come in four types, from simple to complex. Mastering sentence variety makes your English sound mature and professional instead of childish and robotic.

---

## The Four Sentence Types

| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Simple** | 1 independent clause | I like coffee. |
| **Compound** | 2 independent clauses joined | I like coffee, **and** she likes tea. |
| **Complex** | 1 independent + 1+ dependent clauses | **Although** I like coffee, I drink tea at night. |
| **Compound-Complex** | 2+ independent + 1+ dependent | **Although** I like coffee, I drink tea, **but** she always has coffee. |

---

## 1. Simple Sentences

One independent clause = one subject + one verb pair (minimum).

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| S + V | Birds **fly**. |
| S + V + O | I **like** coffee. |
| S + V + C (complement) | She **is** a teacher. |
| S + V + O + O | He **gave** me a gift. |
| S + V + O + C | They **elected** him president. |

> Simple doesn't mean short. "The old man with the red hat walked slowly to the store on the corner." — still a simple sentence (one main clause).

---

## 2. Compound Sentences

Two independent clauses joined by:

### Coordinating Conjunctions: FANBOYS

| Conjunction | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| **F**or | Because | I left, **for** I was tired. |
| **A**nd | Addition | I ate, **and** I drank. |
| **N**or | Negative addition | I don't smoke, **nor** do I drink. |
| **B**ut | Contrast | I called, **but** nobody answered. |
| **O**r | Choice | Hurry, **or** we'll be late. |
| **Y**et | Unexpected contrast | He's rich, **yet** he's unhappy. |
| **S**o | Result | I was hungry, **so** I ate. |

### Semicolons

Two closely related independent clauses: "I love coffee; she prefers tea."

### Conjunctive Adverbs

`however`, `therefore`, `moreover`, `meanwhile`, `otherwise`

> "I wanted to go; **however**, I was too tired."

---

## 3. Complex Sentences

One independent clause + one or more dependent clauses.

### Dependent Clause Starters

| Type | Starters | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| Time | when, while, after, before, as soon as | **When** I arrived, she was waiting. |
| Reason | because, since, as | I stayed home **because** it was raining. |
| Condition | if, unless, provided that | **If** it rains, we'll stay inside. |
| Contrast | although, even though, whereas | **Although** I was tired, I kept working. |
| Purpose | so that, in order that | I left early **so that** I wouldn't be late. |

> **Punctuation:** When dependent clause comes FIRST → use comma. When it comes SECOND → no comma.
> "**When I arrived**, she was waiting." vs "She was waiting **when I arrived**."

---

## 4. Compound-Complex Sentences

Two+ independent clauses + one+ dependent clauses.

> "**Although** I was tired, I went to the party, **and** I stayed until midnight."
> └── dependent ──┘  └── independent ──┘  └── independent ──┘

---

## ⚠️ Common Sentence Errors

| Error | ❌ | ✅ |
|-------|---|---|
| **Run-on** | I love coffee she loves tea. | I love coffee**, and** she loves tea. |
| **Comma splice** | I love coffee, she loves tea. | I love coffee**.** She loves tea. |
| **Fragment** | Because I was tired. | **I went to bed** because I was tired. |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai uses `และ` (and) much more loosely than English. Long Thai sentences connected with `และ` become run-ons in English.

| Thai Style | ❌ English | ✅ English |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| เช้านี้ฉันตื่นสายและไม่ได้กินข้าวและรีบไปทำงาน | This morning I woke up late and didn't eat breakfast and rushed to work. | This morning I woke up late**, so I** didn't eat breakfast **and** rushed to work. |
