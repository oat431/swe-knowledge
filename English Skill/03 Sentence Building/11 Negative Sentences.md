# Negative Sentences

Making a sentence negative in English is more than just adding "no." The structure changes depending on the verb and tense.

---

## The Basic Patterns

| Verb Type | Structure | Example |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| **Be** | S + be + **not** | She **is not** tired. |
| **Modals** | S + modal + **not** + V1 | I **cannot** swim. |
| **Other verbs** | S + do/does/did + **not** + V1 | I **do not** like it. |

---

## 1. Negating "Be"

| Tense | Affirmative | Negative | Contraction |
|-------|------------|----------|-------------|
| Present | I am | I am **not** | I'm not |
| | He is | He is **not** | He isn't / He's not |
| | They are | They are **not** | They aren't / They're not |
| Past | I was | I was **not** | I wasn't |
| | They were | They were **not** | They weren't |

---

## 2. Negating with Do/Does/Did

For all verbs except `be`, modals, and perfect tenses:

| Tense | Affirmative | Negative |
|-------|------------|----------|
| Present (I/you/we/they) | I like it. | I **don't** like it. |
| Present (he/she/it) | She likes it. | She **doesn't** like it. |
| Past | I liked it. | I **didn't** like it. |

⚠️ After `don't/doesn't/didn't`, the main verb returns to **V1**:
> She **likes** → She **doesn't like** (NOT "doesn't likes")
> I **went** → I **didn't go** (NOT "didn't went")

---

## 3. Negating Modals

| Modal | Negative | Contraction |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| can | cannot / can not | **can't** |
| could | could not | **couldn't** |
| will | will not | **won't** |
| would | would not | **wouldn't** |
| should | should not | **shouldn't** |
| must | must not | **mustn't** |
| may | may not | (no contraction) |
| might | might not | mightn't (rare) |

---

## 4. Negating Perfect Tenses

| Tense | Negative |
|-------|----------|
| Present Perfect | I **haven't** finished. / She **hasn't** arrived. |
| Past Perfect | I **hadn't** seen it before. |

---

## 5. Other Negative Words

| Word | Example | Note |
|------|---------|------|
| **never** | I **never** eat meat. | = not ever |
| **nothing** | I saw **nothing**. | = not anything |
| **nobody / no one** | **Nobody** called. | = not anybody |
| **nowhere** | I have **nowhere** to go. | = not anywhere |
| **neither...nor** | I like **neither** coffee **nor** tea. | = not coffee and not tea |
| **hardly / barely / scarcely** | I **hardly** know him. | Almost negative — means "almost not" |

### Double Negatives ❌

English does **not** allow double negatives:

| ❌ Wrong (common in Thai-influenced English) | ✅ Correct |
|---------------------------------------------|-----------|
| I don't have nothing. | I **don't** have **anything**. / I have **nothing**. |
| She didn't see nobody. | She **didn't** see **anybody**. / She saw **nobody**. |
| I can't hardly wait. | I **can hardly** wait. |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai uses `ไม่` (mâi) before the verb — simple and consistent. English is not.

| Thai | ❌ | ✅ |
|------|---|---|
| ฉันไม่ชอบ | I no like. | I **don't** like. |
| เขาไม่ไปเมื่อวาน | He no go yesterday. | He **didn't go** yesterday. |
| ฉันไม่เคยไป | I no ever go. | I've **never** been. |
| ฉันไม่มีอะไร | I don't have nothing. | I **don't** have **anything**. |
