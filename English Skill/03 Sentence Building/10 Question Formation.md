# Question Formation

Asking questions correctly is harder than answering them. English has strict word order rules for questions — get them wrong and you sound like a beginner.

---

## Three Question Types

| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Yes/No** | Auxiliary + S + V...? | **Do** you like coffee? |
| **Wh- (Information)** | Wh-word + Auxiliary + S + V...? | **Where do** you live? |
| **Tag** | Statement + tag? | You like coffee, **don't you**? |

---

## 1. Yes/No Questions

### With "Be" Verb

Just move `be` to the front:
> She **is** tired. → **Is** she tired?

### With Other Verbs

Add `do/does/did` at the front:

| Tense | Statement | Question |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| Present (I/you/we/they) | You like coffee. | **Do** you like coffee? |
| Present (he/she/it) | She likes coffee. | **Does** she like coffee? |
| Past | They went home. | **Did** they go home? |

⚠️ After `does/did`, the main verb goes back to **V1** (base form):
> She **likes** → Does she **like**? (NOT "Does she likes?")
> He **went** → Did he **go**? (NOT "Did he went?")

### With Modals / Perfect

| Statement | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| She **can** swim. | **Can** she swim? |
| They **have** arrived. | **Have** they arrived? |
| You **will** come. | **Will** you come? |

---

## 2. Wh- Questions

| Wh-word | Asks about | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Who** | Person | Who called you? |
| **Whom** | Person (object, formal) | Whom did you see? |
| **What** | Thing / Action | What do you want? |
| **Where** | Place | Where do you live? |
| **When** | Time | When did you arrive? |
| **Why** | Reason | Why are you late? |
| **Which** | Choice | Which one do you prefer? |
| **Whose** | Possession | Whose book is this? |
| **How** | Manner / Method | How did you get here? |
| **How + adj/adv** | Degree | How old are you? How often? How far? |

### Who vs Whom (Subject vs Object Questions)

| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Subject question (Who = subject) | Who + V...? (NO auxiliary) | **Who called** you? |
| Object question (Who = object) | Who + aux + S + V...? | **Who did** you call? |

> "Who called?" = someone called. "Who did you call?" = you called someone. 
> **Who** asks about the doer. **Whom** asks about the receiver (formal).

---

## 3. Tag Questions

Mini-questions added to the end of a statement.

**Rule:** Positive statement → negative tag. Negative statement → positive tag.

| Statement | Tag | Meaning |
|-----------|-----|---------|
| You like coffee, | **don't you**? | Expecting confirmation |
| She isn't coming, | **is she**? | Checking |
| They've finished, | **haven't they**? | Expecting "yes" |
| You won't forget, | **will you**? | Reminder |

### Special Cases

| Statement | Tag |
|-----------|-----|
| I'm right, | **aren't I**? (not "amn't I") |
| Let's go, | **shall we**? |
| Open the door, | **will you**? (imperative) |
| Nobody came, | **did they**? |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai question particles (ไหม, หรือเปล่า, เหรอ) go at the END. English inverts word order at the BEGINNING.

| Thai Question | ❌ | ✅ |
|--------------|---|---|
| คุณชอบกาแฟไหม? | You like coffee? | **Do** you like coffee? |
| เขาไปไหน? | He went where? | **Where did** he go? |
| คุณทำอะไร? | You do what? | **What do** you do? |

> In casual speech, "You like coffee?" with rising tone works. But in writing, tests, or professional English → always use the auxiliary.
