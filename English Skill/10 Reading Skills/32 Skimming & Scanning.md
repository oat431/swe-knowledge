# Skimming & Scanning

Two essential reading speed techniques. IELTS gives you 60 minutes for 40 questions across 3 passages (~2,500 words). TOEFL gives 54–72 minutes for ~30 questions across 2–3 passages. You cannot read every word.

---

## Skimming — Getting the Gist

**Goal:** Understand the main idea and structure in 2–3 minutes.

### How to Skim

| Step | What to Read | Time |
|------|-------------|------|
| 1 | **Title** — tells you the topic | 5 sec |
| 2 | **First paragraph** — usually introduces the main idea | 30 sec |
| 3 | **First sentence of each body paragraph** — the topic sentence | 60 sec |
| 4 | **Last paragraph** — usually summarizes or concludes | 30 sec |
| 5 | **Any bold/italic words, headings, lists** | 15 sec |

### What to Skip While Skimming

- Examples and details
- Long quotations
- Data and statistics (just note they exist)
- Descriptive language

### After Skimming, You Should Know:

1. What is the **topic**?
2. What is the **main argument or idea**?
3. How many **sections** are there?
4. What is the **author's purpose** (to inform, argue, describe)?

---

## Scanning — Finding Specific Information

**Goal:** Locate a specific fact, name, date, or keyword without reading the whole passage.

### How to Scan

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | **Read the question first** — know what you're looking for |
| 2 | **Identify keywords** — names, dates, numbers, capitalized words |
| 3 | **Move your eyes quickly** down the passage — don't read sentences |
| 4 | **Stop when you spot the keyword** — read the surrounding sentence |
| 5 | **Confirm the answer** — make sure it actually answers the question |

### Scannable Information

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Names** | Einstein, Bangkok, World Health Organization |
| **Dates** | 2020, the 19th century, since 1995 |
| **Numbers** | 50%, $3 million, 200 participants |
| **Capitalized words** | Theory of Relativity, Industrial Revolution |
| **Unusual words** | photosynthesis, mitochondria (likely key terms) |

---

## Combining Skimming + Scanning

The most effective reading strategy uses both:

```
Step 1: SKIM the passage (2-3 minutes)
        → Understand structure and main ideas

Step 2: READ the question
        → Identify what you need to find

Step 3: SCAN for the answer
        → Locate the relevant section

Step 4: READ that section carefully
        → Confirm your answer
```

---

## Speed vs. Comprehension Balance

| Approach | When to Use |
|----------|-------------|
| **Skim only** | "What is the main idea?" questions |
| **Scan only** | "When did X happen?" / "According to paragraph 2..." |
| **Careful read** | Inference questions, author's purpose, tone |

> **Common mistake:** Reading the entire passage carefully before looking at questions. This wastes time. Always skim first, then go to questions.

## Practice Exercise

Read this paragraph and answer in 15 seconds:

> "The global population reached 8 billion in November 2022, according to the United Nations. India is projected to surpass China as the world's most populous country by 2023. The rate of population growth has been declining since the 1960s, when it peaked at 2.1% per year."

| Question | Technique | Answer |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| When did the global population reach 8 billion? | Scan | November 2022 |
| What is the main idea? | Skim | Global population reached 8 billion and growth is slowing |
| What organization reported this? | Scan | United Nations |

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| Trap | Problem | Fix |
|------|---------|-----|
| Reading every word | Too slow — you run out of time | Practice skimming: set a 2-minute timer per passage |
| Translating in your head | Slows you down dramatically | Read in English, think in English — force it |
| Ignoring paragraph structure | Missing the topic sentences | Always read the first sentence of each paragraph first |
| Not reading the question first | You don't know what to look for | Always read questions before the passage |
