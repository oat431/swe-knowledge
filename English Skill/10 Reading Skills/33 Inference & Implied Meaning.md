# Inference & Implied Meaning

Some questions don't test what the passage **says** — they test what the passage **implies**. This is the hardest reading skill: understanding meaning that isn't explicitly stated.

---

## What Is Inference?

| Type | Example | How to Find It |
|------|---------|---------------|
| **Explicit** (stated) | "The temperature dropped to -10°C." | Just read it. |
| **Implicit** (implied) | "The temperature dropped to -10°C. The pipes burst." | Infer: The cold caused the pipes to burst. |

The answer isn't written word-for-word. You combine the information and **logically deduce** the meaning.

---

## Common Inference Question Types

### IELTS

| Question Type | Example |
|---------------|---------|
| **Inference** | "What can be inferred from paragraph 3?" |
| **Author's purpose** | "Why does the author mention X?" |
| **Tone/Attitude** | "The writer's attitude toward X is..." |
| **Implication** | "It is implied that..." |

### TOEFL

| Question Type | Example |
|---------------|---------|
| **Inference** | "What can be inferred from paragraph 2 about X?" |
| **Rhetorical purpose** | "Why does the author mention X?" |
| **Negative inference** | "Which of the following is NOT mentioned?" |

---

## How to Answer Inference Questions

### Step-by-Step Process

```
1. Read the question — identify which paragraph or section
2. Re-read that section carefully
3. Look for CLUES — facts that suggest something
4. Connect the clues logically
5. Eliminate answers that are:
   - Too extreme (always, never, all, none)
   - Not supported by any text
   - Directly stated (not an inference — too easy)
6. Choose the answer that MUST be true based on the clues
```

### Example

> **Passage:** "Sales of electric vehicles in Norway reached 80% of new car sales in 2022. The government has offered generous tax incentives for EV buyers since 2012. Meanwhile, petrol stations in rural areas have been closing at an increasing rate."

> **Question:** What can be inferred about Norway's transport policy?

| Answer | Analysis |
|--------|----------|
| A) Norway banned petrol cars in 2022. | ❌ Not stated. Too extreme. |
| B) The government actively encourages EV adoption. | ✅ Supported by "generous tax incentives" + 80% sales |
| C) All Norwegians prefer electric vehicles. | ❌ Too extreme. "80% of new sales" ≠ "all prefer" |
| D) Petrol stations will all close by 2030. | ❌ Not supported. "Closing at increasing rate" ≠ "all close" |

---

## Key Inference Rules

### ✅ A Good Inference
- Is supported by at least one piece of evidence in the text
- Is a logical conclusion, not a wild guess
- Doesn't go beyond what the evidence supports

### ❌ A Bad Inference
- Has no evidence in the text
- Is too extreme (always, never, completely)
- Is directly stated in the text (that's not inference — it's finding)
- Uses outside knowledge that contradicts the passage

## Author's Purpose Questions

| Purpose | Signal Words | Example Question |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| **To inform** | "studies show," "according to," "research indicates" | "The author's main purpose is to..." |
| **To persuade** | "should," "must," "it is essential that" | "The author argues that..." |
| **To compare** | "however," "on the other hand," "unlike" | "The author compares X and Y in order to..." |
| **To explain** | "this means," "in other words," "for example" | "The author mentions X to illustrate..." |

## Tone / Attitude Questions

| Positive | Neutral | Negative |
|----------|---------|----------|
| enthusiastic | objective | critical |
| supportive | informative | skeptical |
| optimistic | descriptive | concerned |
| admiring | analytical | dismissive |
| encouraging | balanced | pessimistic |

### How to Identify Tone

| Clue | Suggests |
|------|----------|
| "brilliant," "remarkable," "outstanding" | Positive |
| "problematic," "concerning," "alarming" | Negative |
| "data shows," "research indicates" | Neutral/Objective |
| "we must," "it is imperative" | Urgent/Persuasive |
| "may," "might," "could potentially" | Cautious/Uncertain |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| Trap | Problem | Fix |
|------|---------|-----|
| Answering from personal knowledge | "I know EVs are good, so the answer is B" | Only use evidence from the passage |
| Choosing the directly stated answer | "The passage says tax incentives — that's my answer!" | Inference questions want what's IMPLIED, not stated |
| Picking extreme answers | "ALL Norwegians prefer EVs" | Look for words like "all," "never," "always" — usually wrong |
| Not re-reading the relevant section | Going from memory | Always go back and re-read the specific paragraph |
