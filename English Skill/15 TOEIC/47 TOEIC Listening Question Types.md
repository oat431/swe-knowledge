# TOEIC Listening Question Types

TOEIC Listening has **100 questions** in 4 parts (~45 minutes). The audio plays only once. Each part has a specific strategy.

---

## Part 1: Photographs (6 questions)

You see a **photo** and hear 4 statements. Choose the one that describes the photo.

### Strategy

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Look at the photo before the audio starts — identify what's happening |
| 2 | Listen for **accurate** descriptions, not plausible ones |
| 3 | Eliminate statements with wrong verbs, wrong prepositions, or wrong numbers |

### What to Listen For

| Trap | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Wrong verb** | Photo shows someone sitting. Audio says "She is standing." ❌ |
| **Wrong preposition** | Photo shows a book ON the table. Audio says "under." ❌ |
| **Wrong number** | Photo shows 3 people. Audio says "two." ❌ |
| **Sounds right but isn't** | "The man is reading a letter." (Photo shows a newspaper.) ❌ |

### Common Photo Types

| Photo Type | Listen For |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Office** | People working, meeting, typing, discussing |
| **Outdoor** | Walking, standing, waiting, construction |
| **Store/Restaurant** | Shopping, ordering, paying, serving |
| **Factory/Warehouse** | Manufacturing, operating, loading, packing |

---

## Part 2: Question-Response (25 questions)

You hear a **question** and 3 responses. Choose the best response.

### Strategy

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Focus on the **first word** of the question (Who, What, Where, When, How) |
| 2 | Match the response type to the question type |
| 3 | Listen for **related words** — distractors use words from the question |

### Question Type → Response Type

| Question Starts With | Answer Should Be... | Example |
|---------------------|---------------------|---------|
| **Who** | A person | Q: "Who approved the budget?" A: "The finance director did." |
| **What** | A thing / action | Q: "What time is the meeting?" A: "It's at 2 PM." |
| **Where** | A place | Q: "Where is the conference room?" A: "On the third floor." |
| **When** | A time | Q: "When is the deadline?" A: "Next Friday." |
| **How many** | A number | Q: "How many people are coming?" A: "About twenty." |
| **How much** | An amount / price | Q: "How much does it cost?" A: "Fifty dollars." |
| **Why** | A reason | Q: "Why was the meeting canceled?" A: "The director was sick." |
| **Which** | A choice | Q: "Which report do you need?" A: "The quarterly one." |

### The #1 Trap: Similar-Sounding Words

| ❌ Trap | Why It's Wrong |
|---------|---------------|
| Q: "Where is the **file**?" A: "I'll **fill** it out." | **file** ≠ **fill** — similar sound, different meaning |
| Q: "When is the **meeting**?" A: "He's **meting** the deadline." | **meeting** ≠ **meting** — sounds similar |
| Q: "Who **wrote** the report?" A: "I'll **route** it to you." | **wrote** ≠ **route** — distractor |

> **Rule:** If a response uses a word that sounds like the question, it's usually WRONG. That's a distractor.

---

## Part 3: Conversations (39 questions, 13 conversations)

You hear a **conversation between 2-3 people** and answer 3 questions per conversation.

### Strategy

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Read the 3 questions **before** the audio starts |
| 2 | Listen for specific answers to those questions |
| 3 | Note: questions follow the order of the conversation |

### Common Question Types

| Question Type | What to Listen For |
|--------------|-------------------|
| **Main idea** | "What are they discussing?" — listen to the opening |
| **Specific detail** | "When will the delivery arrive?" — listen for numbers/dates |
| **Inference** | "What will the woman probably do next?" — listen to the end |
| **Opinion** | "What does the man think about...?" — listen for "I think," "I believe" |
| **Purpose** | "Why is the man calling?" — listen to the opening |

### The 3 Questions Pattern

| Question | Usually About |
|----------|--------------|
| **Question 1** | The opening — why they're talking |
| **Question 2** | A detail in the middle |
| **Question 3** | The ending — what happens next, or a conclusion |

---

## Part 4: Short Talks (30 questions, 10 talks)

You hear a **monologue** (one person speaking) and answer 3 questions per talk.

### Common Talk Types

| Type | Context | Listen For |
|------|---------|-----------|
| **Announcement** | Company news, schedule change | What changed, when, who is affected |
| **Voicemail** | Someone leaving a message | Who called, why, what they want |
| **Presentation** | Product introduction, report | Key features, numbers, recommendations |
| **Tour** | Office, building, factory | Locations, purposes, schedules |
| **Advertisement** | Product/service promotion | Price, features, how to order |

### Strategy

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Read questions before audio |
| 2 | Listen for the **main purpose** first (usually Q1) |
| 3 | Listen for **specific details** (usually Q2-Q3) |
| 4 | Pay extra attention to the **ending** — often contains the final answer |

---

## General Listening Tips

### Before the Audio

- Read the questions and options (Parts 3-4)
- Predict what you'll hear
- Identify keywords to listen for

### During the Audio

- Don't stare at the test booklet — listen with your ears, not your eyes
- If you miss an answer, guess and move on immediately
- Don't change your answer unless you're 100% sure

### After Each Section

- Transfer answers immediately (TOEIC uses a separate answer sheet)
- Don't wait until the end — you might run out of time

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| Trap | Problem | Fix |
|------|---------|-----|
| Translating in your head | By the time you translate, the audio moved on | Practice listening in English without translating |
| Falling for sound-alikes | "Where is the **suit**?" → "I'll **suit** it." (wrong) | The correct answer usually doesn't repeat the question word |
| Not reading questions first | You don't know what to listen for | Always preview questions before audio starts (Parts 3-4) |
| Panicking on Part 2 | Questions come fast — 25 in a row | Stay calm — guess if stuck and move on |
