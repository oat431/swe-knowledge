# Business Vocabulary

TOEIC tests **workplace English** — meetings, emails, travel, reports, schedules. If you only know academic vocabulary, TOEIC will feel foreign. This file covers the business words that appear most frequently.

---

## Why Business Vocabulary Matters

| Test | Focus |
|------|-------|
| **IELTS** | Academic + general English |
| **TOEFL** | Academic English (university) |
| **TOEIC** | **Workplace + business English** |

> TOEIC doesn't test academic vocabulary like "hypothesis" or "methodology." It tests words like "deadline," "agenda," and "quarterly."

---

## Core Business Categories

### 1. Office & Workplace

| Word | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **agenda** | list of topics for a meeting | "Let's review the **agenda** before we start." |
| **minutes** | written record of a meeting | "Who will take the **minutes** today?" |
| **deadline** | final date to complete something | "The **deadline** for the report is Friday." |
| **memo** | internal message/document | "Please send a **memo** to all staff." |
| **cubicle** | small enclosed workspace | "His **cubicle** is on the third floor." |
| **branch** | local office of a company | "The company has **branches** in 10 countries." |
| **headquarters (HQ)** | main office | "The **headquarters** is in Tokyo." |
| **supervisor** | person who manages your work | "My **supervisor** approved the request." |
| **colleague** | person you work with | "She discussed it with her **colleagues**." |
| **subordinate** | person below you in rank | "The manager briefed her **subordinates**." |
| **client** | customer (formal) | "We met with a **client** this morning." |
| **vendor** | company that sells to you | "The **vendor** delivered the supplies." |

### 2. Meetings & Communication

| Word | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **schedule** | plan of events/times | "What's on the **schedule** for today?" |
| **postpone** | delay to a later time | "The meeting was **postponed** until Monday." |
| **cancel** | stop something planned | "They had to **cancel** the conference." |
| **reschedule** | change the time | "Can we **reschedule** for next week?" |
| **confirm** | verify/affirm | "Please **confirm** your attendance." |
| **notify** | inform officially | "Employees will be **notified** by email." |
| **briefing** | short informational meeting | "The **briefing** will last 15 minutes." |
| **proposal** | formal suggestion/plan | "She submitted a **proposal** for the project." |
| **feedback** | response/reaction | "We welcome your **feedback**." |
| **negotiate** | discuss to reach agreement | "They **negotiated** the contract for weeks." |

### 3. Finance & Budget

| Word | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **budget** | planned spending amount | "We need to stay within **budget**." |
| **revenue** | money earned | "Annual **revenue** increased by 15%." |
| **expense** | cost/spending | "Travel **expenses** will be reimbursed." |
| **profit** | money gained after costs | "The company made a **profit** of $2 million." |
| **loss** | money lost | "The division reported a **loss** this quarter." |
| **quarter** | 3-month business period (Q1-Q4) | "Sales rose in the third **quarter**." |
| **invoice** | bill for goods/services | "Please send the **invoice** to accounting." |
| **receipt** | proof of payment | "Keep your **receipt** for reimbursement." |
| **discount** | reduced price | "Employees get a 20% **discount**." |
| **refund** | money returned | "You can request a **refund** within 30 days." |
| **audit** | official financial review | "The **audit** found no errors." |

### 4. Sales & Marketing

| Word | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **launch** | start/release a product | "We'll **launch** the new product in March." |
| **campaign** | organized effort to promote | "The marketing **campaign** was a success." |
| **target** | goal/objective | "We reached our sales **target**." |
| **market share** | portion of the market | "Our **market share** grew by 5%." |
| **promotion** | advertising / moving up in rank | "She received a **promotion** to manager." |
| **brand** | company's product identity | "The **brand** is well-known in Asia." |
| **survey** | questionnaire for customers | "We conducted a customer **survey**." |
| **brochure** | printed information booklet | "Pick up a **brochure** at the front desk." |

### 5. Human Resources

| Word | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **hire** | give someone a job | "The company plans to **hire** 50 people." |
| **recruit** | find new employees | "They're **recruiting** for the IT department." |
| **resume / CV** | document of your experience | "Please submit your **resume** by Friday." |
| **benefits** | extra perks (health, vacation) | "The **benefits** include health insurance." |
| **probation** | trial period for new employees | "New hires are on **probation** for 3 months." |
| **resign** | quit voluntarily | "He **resigned** to pursue other opportunities." |
| **terminate** | fire/end employment | "The company **terminated** the contract." |
| **overtime** | extra hours beyond normal | "She worked **overtime** to finish the project." |
| **leave** | time off work | "She's on maternity **leave**." |

### 6. Travel & Logistics

| Word | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **itinerary** | travel plan | "Here's your **itinerary** for the trip." |
| **reservation** | booking | "I made a **reservation** at the hotel." |
| **departure** | leaving | "The **departure** time is 8:00 AM." |
| **arrival** | arriving | "The **arrival** gate is B12." |
| **layover** | stop between flights | "We have a 3-hour **layover** in Seoul." |
| **accommodation** | place to stay | "The **accommodation** is near the office." |
| **reimburse** | pay back money spent | "The company will **reimburse** your taxi fare." |
| **shipment** | goods being sent | "The **shipment** will arrive Monday." |
| **inventory** | list of stock/goods | "We need to check the **inventory**." |

### 7. Technology & Equipment

| Word | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **software** | computer programs | "The new **software** is easier to use." |
| **hardware** | physical computer parts | "We need to upgrade the **hardware**." |
| **malfunction** | not working properly | "The printer **malfunctioned** again." |
| **upgrade** | improve to a better version | "It's time to **upgrade** our system." |
| **maintenance** | regular upkeep | "The server is down for **maintenance**." |
| **compatible** | works together | "Is this **compatible** with Windows?" |

## Common TOEIC Email Phrases

| Phrase | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| "I am writing to inquire about..." | I want to ask about... |
| "Please find attached..." | Look at the attached file... |
| "I would like to confirm that..." | I want to verify... |
| "Thank you for your prompt response." | Thanks for replying quickly. |
| "Please do not hesitate to contact me." | Feel free to reach out. |
| "I look forward to hearing from you." | I await your reply. |
| "Apologies for the delay in responding." | Sorry for the late reply. |
| "As per your request..." | As you asked... |

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| Trap | Problem | Fix |
|------|---------|-----|
| Confusing "deadline" and "dateline" | "Dateline" = international date line | **Deadline** = last day to finish something |
| Using "boss" in formal writing | Too informal for TOEIC | Use **supervisor**, **manager**, **director** |
| Confusing "borrow" and "lend" | Thai uses ยืม for both | **Borrow** = take. **Lend** = give. "Can I **borrow** $10?" / "Can you **lend** me $10?" |
| Using "price" for cost/expense | Not always interchangeable | **Price** = what you pay. **Cost** = what it takes to make. **Expense** = business spending. |
