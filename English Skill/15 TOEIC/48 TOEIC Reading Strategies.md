# TOEIC Reading Strategies

TOEIC Reading has **100 questions** in 3 parts (~75 minutes). Time pressure is intense — you have **45 seconds per question** on average. Speed is everything.

---

## Part 5: Incomplete Sentences (30 questions)

A sentence with a **blank**. Choose the word/phrase that best completes it.

### Strategy

| Step | Action | Time |
|------|--------|------|
| 1 | Read the sentence with the blank | 5 sec |
| 2 | Identify what type of word is needed (noun? verb? adjective?) | 5 sec |
| 3 | Check grammar (tense, subject-verb agreement) | 5 sec |
| 4 | Choose and move on | 5 sec |

> **Target: 20 seconds per question.** Save time for Parts 6 and 7.

### Common Question Types

| Type | What's Tested | Example |
|------|--------------|---------|
| **Vocabulary** | Word meaning | "The company _____ a new policy." (adopted/adapted/adjusted) |
| **Grammar** | Tense, agreement, word form | "Each employee _____ required to attend." (is/are/was/were) |
| **Collocation** | Natural word pairs | "We need to _____ a decision." (make/do/take) |
| **Preposition** | Correct preposition | "The report is due _____ Friday." (in/on/at) |
| **Word form** | Noun vs verb vs adjective | "The _____ was approved by the board." (propose/proposal/proposed) |

### Grammar Points Frequently Tested

| Topic | Example |
|-------|---------|
| **Subject-Verb Agreement** | "The manager, along with the staff, **is** attending." (not "are") |
| **Tense** | "She **has worked** here since 2019." (not "worked") |
| **Conditionals** | "If the budget **is** approved, we will proceed." |
| **Passive voice** | "The report **was submitted** yesterday." |
| **Relative clauses** | "The employee **who** completed the training received a certificate." |
| **Comparatives** | "This quarter's results are **better** than last quarter's." |
| **Gerunds vs Infinitives** | "She suggested **holding** a meeting." (not "to hold") |

## Part 6: Text Completion (16 questions, 4 passages)

A **short text** (email, letter, notice) with 4 blanks. Choose the best word/phrase for each blank.

### Strategy

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Read the entire text first — understand the context |
| 2 | Fill in the blanks you're sure about first |
| 3 | For vocabulary blanks — check what fits the meaning |
| 4 | For sentence blanks — check what fits the flow |

### Common Blank Types

| Blank Type | What to Check |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Word** (vocabulary/grammar) | Same as Part 5 — meaning and grammar |
| **Sentence** (whole sentence missing) | What does the text need? A reason? A transition? A closing? |

### Text Types

| Type | Features |
|------|----------|
| **Email** | Opening greeting, request, details, closing |
| **Memo** | To/From/Date/Subject, announcement, action needed |
| **Notice** | Announcement, dates, instructions |
| **Letter** | Formal business letter format |

## Part 7: Reading Comprehension (54 questions, single + double passages)

**Long passages** with 2-5 questions each. This is the hardest part — and the most time-consuming.

### Single Passages (28 questions)

One passage, 2-4 questions.

### Double Passages (26 questions)

Two related passages (e.g., an email + a reply), 4-5 questions.

### Strategy

| Step | Action | Time |
|------|--------|------|
| 1 | Read the questions first — know what to look for | 30 sec |
| 2 | Skim the passage — understand the main idea | 60 sec |
| 3 | Find specific answers by scanning for keywords | 90 sec |
| 4 | Answer and move on | — |

### Common Question Types

| Type | What to Look For |
|------|-----------------|
| **Main idea** | What is the passage about? — check the opening and closing |
| **Specific detail** | "When is the deadline?" — scan for dates/numbers |
| **Inference** | "What will probably happen?" — combine clues |
| **Vocabulary** | "The word 'submit' is closest in meaning to..." — check context |
| **Purpose** | "Why was this email written?" — check the opening |
| **Who/Where** | "Who should contact Mr. Lee?" — scan for names |

### Double Passage Strategy

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Read Passage 1 first — understand it |
| 2 | Read the questions — identify which passage each question refers to |
| 3 | Read Passage 2 — look for connections to Passage 1 |
| 4 | Answer questions that reference both passages last |

### Passage Types

| Type | What It Is |
|------|-----------|
| **Email** | Business correspondence — requests, replies, confirmations |
| **Letter** | Formal business letter — complaints, inquiries, proposals |
| **Advertisement** | Product/service promotion — features, prices, contact info |
| **Notice/Announcement** | Company news — schedule changes, policies, events |
| **Article** | News or industry article — trends, analysis, opinions |
| **Form/Schedule** | Registration form, meeting schedule, itinerary |
| **Invoice/Receipt** | Financial document — items, prices, totals |

## Time Management for TOEIC Reading

| Part | Questions | Target Time | Time per Question |
|------|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Part 5** | 30 | 10 minutes | ~20 seconds |
| **Part 6** | 16 | 10 minutes | ~40 seconds |
| **Part 7** | 54 | 55 minutes | ~60 seconds |
| **Total** | 100 | 75 minutes | ~45 seconds average |

> **Key insight:** Parts 5 and 6 are fast. Save time there for Part 7, which takes the longest.

### What If You're Running Out of Time?

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Part 5 taking too long | Don't overthink — go with your first instinct |
| Part 6 taking too long | Read the whole text quickly, then fill blanks |
| Part 7 behind schedule | Skip double passages (harder) — do single passages first |
| Last 5 minutes | Guess remaining answers — no penalty |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| Trap | Problem | Fix |
|------|---------|-----|
| Reading every word in Part 7 | Too slow — you run out of time | Skim first, then scan for answers |
| Spending 2+ minutes on one Part 5 question | Steals time from Part 7 | 20 seconds max — move on if unsure |
| Not reading questions first (Part 7) | You don't know what to look for | Always read questions before the passage |
| Confusing "adopted" and "adapted" | Common TOEIC trap | **Adopted** = took/accepted. **Adapted** = adjusted. |
| Ignoring double passages | They're worth the most points | Practice double passage strategy |
