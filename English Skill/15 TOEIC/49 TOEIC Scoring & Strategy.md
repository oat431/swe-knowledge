# TOEIC Scoring & Strategy

Understanding how TOEIC is scored helps you set realistic goals and focus your practice.

---

## TOEIC Test Format

### Listening & Reading Test (the standard TOEIC)

| Section | Questions | Time | Score Range |
|---------|-----------|------|-------------|
| **Listening** | 100 | ~45 min | 5–495 |
| **Reading** | 100 | 75 min | 5–495 |
| **Total** | **200** | **~2 hours** | **10–990** |

> There is no speaking or writing in the standard TOEIC. Some companies require the separate Speaking & Writing test.

### Speaking & Writing Test (separate)

| Section | Tasks | Time | Score Range |
|---------|-------|------|-------------|
| **Speaking** | 11 tasks | ~20 min | 0–200 |
| **Writing** | 8 tasks | ~60 min | 0–200 |
| **Total** | **19 tasks** | **~80 min** | **0–400** |

## Score Levels

### Listening & Reading (10-990)

| Score | Level | What It Means |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| **905-990** | International Professional | Can work in any English environment |
| **785-900** | Working Proficiency Plus | Can handle most business situations |
| **605-780** | Limited Working Proficiency | Can handle routine business tasks |
| **405-600** | Elementary Proficiency | Basic communication in familiar situations |
| **255-400** | Survival Level | Can understand simple instructions |
| **10-250** | Beginner | Minimal English ability |

## IELTS vs TOEFL vs TOEIC

| Feature | IELTS | TOEFL | TOEIC |
|---------|-------|-------|-------|
| **Purpose** | Study abroad, immigration | Study abroad (US focus) | Employment, promotion |
| **English type** | Academic + General | Academic | Business / Workplace |
| **Sections** | L, R, W, S | L, R, W, S | L, R (standard test) |
| **Duration** | 2 hrs 45 min | ~3 hours | ~2 hours |
| **Score range** | 0-9 (bands) | 0-120 | 10-990 |
| **Valid for** | 2 years | 2 years | 2 years |
| **Best for** | Study in UK/AU/Canada | Study in US | Jobs in Thailand/Asia |

## TOEIC-Specific Strategy

### Before the Test

| Action | Why |
|--------|-----|
| Take 2-3 full practice tests | Know the format and your weak areas |
| Practice with a timer | Build speed — TOEIC is fast |
| Study business vocabulary (see [[46 Business Vocabulary]]) | The #1 differentiator from IELTS/TOEFL |
| Listen to business English daily | Podcasts, news (BBC Business, CNBC) |
| Review grammar fundamentals | Parts 5-6 test grammar heavily |

### During the Test: Listening

| Part | Strategy |
|------|----------|
| **Part 1 (Photos)** | Look at photo before audio. Eliminate wrong descriptions. |
| **Part 2 (Q-Response)** | Focus on the first word of the question. Don't pick sound-alikes. |
| **Part 3 (Conversations)** | Read questions before audio. Listen for specific answers. |
| **Part 4 (Short Talks)** | Same as Part 3. Pay attention to the ending. |

### During the Test: Reading

| Part | Strategy |
|------|----------|
| **Part 5 (Sentences)** | 20 seconds max per question. Go with your first instinct. |
| **Part 6 (Texts)** | Read the whole text first. Fill easy blanks first. |
| **Part 7 (Passages)** | Read questions first. Skim passage. Scan for answers. |

### Answering Strategy

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| **Never leave a blank** | No penalty for wrong answers — always guess |
| **Don't change answers** | Your first instinct is usually right (unless you're sure) |
| **Mark and move on** | Come back to hard questions if time allows |
| **Fill the answer sheet as you go** | Don't wait until the end — you might run out of time |

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| Trap | Problem | Fix |
|------|---------|-----|
| Studying IELTS material for TOEIC | Different vocabulary and format | Use TOEIC-specific prep books (Barron's, ETS official) |
| Ignoring Part 2 | 25 questions — fast and tricky | Practice Part 2 daily — it's the easiest to improve |
| Not practicing with a timer | Real test is fast — 20 sec/question for Part 5 | Always practice under time pressure |
| Focusing only on Listening | Reading is worth the same score | Split practice equally between L and R |
| Not knowing business vocabulary | Can't understand the context | Study [[46 Business Vocabulary]] thoroughly |
