# Time Management

Both IELTS and TOEFL are timed tests. Poor time management is the #1 reason students score lower than their actual ability.

---

## IELTS Time Management

### IELTS Academic Test Format

| Section | Time | Questions | Time per Question |
|---------|------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Listening** | 30 min + 10 min transfer | 40 questions | ~45 sec |
| **Reading** | 60 minutes | 40 questions | 1.5 min |
| **Writing** | 60 minutes | 2 tasks | 20 min (Task 1) + 40 min (Task 2) |
| **Speaking** | 11-14 minutes | 3 parts | Examiner manages time |

### Reading: The Biggest Time Trap

| Passage | Suggested Time | Strategy |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| **Passage 1** (easiest) | 15-17 minutes | Get it done fast — save time for harder passages |
| **Passage 2** (medium) | 18-20 minutes | Standard approach |
| **Passage 3** (hardest) | 20-23 minutes | Spend more time here — it's worth the same marks |

#### Reading Time Budget

```
Total: 60 minutes for 3 passages

Per passage:
├── Skim passage: 2-3 minutes
├── Read questions: 1 minute
├── Find answers: 10-12 minutes
└── Check answers: 2 minutes
```

#### What to Do When Stuck

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Can't find the answer after 2 minutes | Mark it, move on, come back later |
| Between two answers | Pick the one with more evidence, move on |
| Running out of time | Guess remaining answers — no penalty |

### Writing: Task 2 Is Worth More

| Task | Time | Words | Weight |
|------|------|-------|--------|
| **Task 1** | 20 minutes | 150+ | **33%** of writing score |
| **Task 2** | 40 minutes | 250+ | **67%** of writing score |

> **Key rule:** Always finish Task 2. A half-finished Task 2 costs more than a short Task 1.

#### Writing Time Budget

**Task 1 (20 min):**

| Step | Time |
|------|------|
| Analyze the data | 3 min |
| Plan your response | 2 min |
| Write | 13 min |
| Check (grammar, spelling) | 2 min |

**Task 2 (40 min):**

| Step | Time |
|------|------|
| Understand the question | 2 min |
| Plan (brainstorm + outline) | 5 min |
| Write | 28 min |
| Check (grammar, spelling, coherence) | 5 min |

## TOEFL Time Management

### TOEFL iBT Test Format

| Section | Time | Tasks |
|---------|------|-------|
| **Reading** | 54-72 min | 2-3 passages, 10 questions each |
| **Listening** | 41-57 min | 3-4 lectures + 2-3 conversations |
| **Speaking** | 17 min | 4 tasks |
| **Writing** | 50 min | 2 tasks (integrated + independent) |

### Reading Time Budget

| Passage | Time | Strategy |
|---------|------|----------|
| Each passage | ~18 minutes | Skim → Read questions → Find answers |

### Writing Time Budget

**Task 1: Integrated Writing (20 min):**

| Step | Time |
|------|------|
| Review reading notes | 1 min |
| Review listening notes | 1 min |
| Plan (fill in the grid) | 2 min |
| Write | 14 min |
| Check | 2 min |

**Task 2: Independent Writing (30 min):**

| Step | Time |
|------|------|
| Understand the question | 2 min |
| Plan (brainstorm + outline) | 5 min |
| Write | 20 min |
| Check | 3 min |

## General Time Management Rules

### The 2-Minute Rule

If you've spent 2 minutes on a single question, **mark it and move on**. Come back if time allows.

### The Check-Always Rule

Always leave 2-5 minutes to check:
- Spelling errors
- Grammar mistakes (especially [[03 Subject-Verb Agreement]])
- Missing words
- Word count (IELTS: Task 1 = 150+, Task 2 = 250+)

### The Guess Rule

Never leave a blank answer. There's no penalty for wrong answers. If time is running out:
- **Multiple choice:** Pick the most reasonable option
- **True/False/Not Given:** Guess True (most common answer statistically)
- **Matching:** Match what you can, guess the rest

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| Trap | Problem | Fix |
|------|---------|-----|
| Spending too long on one reading question | Running out of time for easy questions | 2-minute rule — move on if stuck |
| Writing Task 2 first | Running out of time for Task 1 | Write Task 1 first (it's shorter and easier) |
| Not checking work | Losing marks to silly mistakes | Always leave 2-5 minutes for checking |
| Panicking during listening | Missing the next audio while panicking | Accept missed answers and keep listening |
| Writing too much in Task 1 | Leaving less time for Task 2 | Task 1: 150-180 words max. Task 2 needs more time. |
