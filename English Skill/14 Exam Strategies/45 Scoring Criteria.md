# Scoring Criteria

Understanding how you're scored helps you focus your practice. Don't prepare blindly — know what the examiners are looking for.

---

## IELTS Scoring

### Overall Band Score

| Band | Level | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| **9** | Expert | Full operational command |
| **8** | Very Good | Occasional inaccuracies |
| **7** | Good | Generally effective command |
| **6** | Competent | Generally effective despite some inaccuracies |
| **5** | Modest | Partial command — copes with overall meaning |
| **4** | Limited | Frequent problems — not able to use complex language |
| **3** | Extremely Limited | Conveys only general meaning in familiar situations |
| **2** | Intermittent | Great difficulty understanding spoken/written English |
| **1** | Non-user | No ability to use the language |

### IELTS Writing — Band Descriptors

| Criterion | Weight | What Examiners Look For |
|-----------|--------|------------------------|
| **Task Achievement** | 25% | Did you answer all parts of the question? Is your position clear? |
| **Coherence & Cohesion** | 25% | Is your essay logically organized? Do you use linking words well? |
| **Lexical Resource** | 25% | Do you use a range of vocabulary? Collocations? Academic words? |
| **Grammatical Range & Accuracy** | 25% | Do you use complex structures? Are your sentences error-free? |

#### Writing Band 7 vs Band 6

| Criterion | Band 6 | Band 7 |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Task Achievement** | Addresses all parts, but some more fully than others | Addresses all parts, clear position throughout |
| **Coherence & Cohesion** | Cohesive but not always appropriate | Logical sequencing, good use of cohesive devices |
| **Lexical Resource** | Adequate range, some errors | Sufficient range, occasional errors in word choice |
| **Grammar** | Mix of simple and complex, some errors | Variety of complex structures, frequent error-free sentences |

### IELTS Speaking — Band Descriptors

| Criterion | Weight | What Examiners Look For |
|-----------|--------|------------------------|
| **Fluency & Coherence** | 25% | Can you speak without long pauses? Is your speech logical? |
| **Lexical Resource** | 25% | Do you use varied vocabulary? Can you paraphrase? |
| **Grammatical Range & Accuracy** | 25% | Do you use complex structures? Are errors systematic? |
| **Pronunciation** | 25% | Can you be understood? Do you use intonation and stress? |

### IELTS Reading & Listening

## TOEFL Scoring

### Overall Score

| Score | Level |
|-------|-------|
| **110-120** | Advanced |
| **94-109** | High-Intermediate |
| **72-93** | Intermediate |
| **42-71** | Low-Intermediate |
| **0-41** | Basic |

### Section Scores (0-30 each)

| Section | High (26-30) | Intermediate (18-25) | Basic (0-17) |
|---------|-------------|---------------------|--------------|
| **Reading** | Understands complex academic texts | Understands main ideas | Struggles with basic texts |
| **Listening** | Understands lectures and conversations | Understands main points | Misses key information |
| **Speaking** | Clear, organized, detailed responses | Generally clear, some gaps | Difficult to understand |
| **Writing** | Well-organized, accurate, detailed | Generally organized, some errors | Poorly organized, many errors |

### TOEFL Writing — Scoring

| Criterion | Integrated (0-5) | Independent (0-5) |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|
| **5 (High)** | Accurately presents all lecture points, well-organized | Well-organized, detailed, varied vocabulary |
| **4 (Fair)** | Mostly accurate, minor omissions | Generally organized, adequate detail |
| **3 (Limited)** | Some inaccuracies, missing points | Some organization, limited detail |
| **2 (Weak)** | Major inaccuracies, poorly organized | Poorly organized, very limited |
| **1 (Very Weak)** | Mostly copied from reading | Extremely limited or off-topic |

### TOEFL Speaking — Scoring

| Criterion | What It Means |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Delivery** | Clear speech, natural pace, good pronunciation |
| **Language Use** | Varied vocabulary, accurate grammar |
| **Topic Development** | Complete responses, logical organization, specific details |

## What Universities Require

| University Level | IELTS | TOEFL |
|-----------------|-------|-------|
| **Top universities** (MIT, Oxford) | 7.0-7.5 | 100-110 |
| **Good universities** (most US/UK/AU) | 6.5 | 80-90 |
| **Foundation / Pathway programs** | 5.5-6.0 | 60-78 |
| **Immigration (Australia, Canada)** | 6.0-7.0 | N/A (IELTS preferred) |

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| Trap | Problem | Fix |
|------|---------|-----|
| Focusing only on grammar | Grammar is only 25% of writing score | Also practice vocabulary, coherence, and task achievement |
| Ignoring pronunciation | 25% of speaking score | Practice stress, intonation, and problem sounds (see [[26 Pronunciation Guide]]) |
| Not knowing the format | Wasting time figuring out instructions | Take practice tests before the real exam |
| Aiming for "perfect" English | Spending years preparing | Most universities accept IELTS 6.5 — that's achievable in 6-12 months |
