# Prepositions

Prepositions show relationships — where something is, when something happens, which direction it moves. They're small words (`in`, `on`, `at`, `to`, `for`, `with`) but getting them wrong immediately marks you as a non-native speaker.

---

## The Big Three: In, On, At

### Time

| Preposition | Use | Example |
|------------|-----|---------|
| **at** | Specific time | at 5pm, at midnight, at lunchtime |
| **on** | Days & dates | on Monday, on June 1st, on my birthday |
| **in** | Longer periods | in June, in 2024, in the morning, in summer |

> at < on < in (specific → general)

### Place

| Preposition | Use | Example |
|------------|-----|---------|
| **at** | Specific point / location | at the door, at the bus stop, at work, at home |
| **on** | Surface / street | on the table, on the wall, on Sukhumvit Road |
| **in** | Enclosed space / area | in the room, in Bangkok, in Thailand |

---

## Time Prepositions

| Preposition | Use | Example |
|------------|-----|---------|
| **since** | From a point in past until now | I've been here **since** 2015. |
| **for** | Duration | I've been here **for** 9 years. |
| **during** | Throughout a period | **During** the meeting, my phone rang. |
| **by** | Deadline (not later than) | Finish it **by** Friday. |
| **until/till** | Up to a point | Wait **until** I come back. |
| **from...to** | Range | I work **from** 9 **to** 5. |
| **before / after** | Sequence | Call me **before** you leave. |
| **within** | Inside a time limit | Reply **within** 24 hours. |

> ⚠️ **Since vs For:** "since" = starting point. "for" = duration. "I've lived here **since** 2020 / **for** 4 years."

## Place & Position Prepositions

| Preposition | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| **above** | Higher than (not touching) | The clock is **above** the door. |
| **over** | Directly above / covering | A bridge goes **over** the river. |
| **below** | Lower than | The temperature is **below** zero. |
| **under** | Directly beneath | The cat is **under** the table. |
| **between** | In the middle of two | Sit **between** John and Mary. |
| **among** | Surrounded by many | She's popular **among** her friends. |
| **next to / beside** | At the side of | The bank is **next to** the post office. |
| **near / close to** | Not far | I live **near** the station. |
| **opposite** | Facing | The cafe is **opposite** the park. |
| **in front of** | Before | The car is **in front of** the house. |
| **behind** | At the back of | The garden is **behind** the house. |

## Direction Prepositions

| Preposition | Example |
|------------|---------|
| **to** | I'm going **to** school. |
| **from** | I came **from** Bangkok. |
| **into** | She walked **into** the room. |
| **out of** | He ran **out of** the building. |
| **onto** | The cat jumped **onto** the table. |
| **toward(s)** | Walk **toward** the station. |
| **through** | We walked **through** the park. |
| **along** | Walk **along** the river. |
| **across** | Swim **across** the lake. |
| **past** | He walked **past** me without saying hello. |

## Other Important Prepositions

| Preposition | Use | Example |
|------------|-----|---------|
| **about** | Topic | We talked **about** the project. |
| **with** | Accompaniment / tool | I went **with** her. / Cut it **with** a knife. |
| **without** | Absence | I can't live **without** coffee. |
| **by** | Method / agent | I go to work **by** bus. / It was written **by** her. |
| **for** | Purpose / recipient | This gift is **for** you. |
| **of** | Belonging / part | The cover **of** the book. |
| **to** | Direction / recipient | Give it **to** me. |

## Preposition-Dependent Words

Some words require specific prepositions — there's no rule, you must memorize them.

### Adjectives + Prepositions

| Adjective | Preposition | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| afraid | **of** | afraid of spiders |
| good/bad | **at** | good at math |
| interested | **in** | interested in history |
| worried | **about** | worried about money |
| famous | **for** | famous for its food |
| different | **from** (UK: to) | different from mine |
| married | **to** | married to a doctor |
| similar | **to** | similar to this one |

### Verbs + Prepositions

| Verb | Preposition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| depend | **on** | It depends on the weather. |
| believe | **in** | I believe in hard work. |
| belong | **to** | This belongs to me. |
| apply | **for** | I applied for the job. |
| wait | **for** | Wait for me. |
| listen | **to** | Listen to the music. |
| look | **at** | Look at that! |
| think | **about / of** | I'm thinking about you. |

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai uses fewer prepositions and some concepts are expressed very differently.

| Thai | ❌ | ✅ |
|------|---|---|
| ฉันอยู่กรุงเทพ | I live in Bangkok. (correct) / I live Bangkok. ❌ | I live **in** Bangkok. |
| ฉันสนใจประวัติศาสตร์ | I'm interested history. | I'm interested **in** history. |
| ฉันรอคุณ | I'm waiting you. | I'm waiting **for** you. |
| แต่งงานกับ... | married with... | married **to**... |

> "Married with" = you and someone else are both married. "Married to" = you are someone's spouse. Very different!
