# Transition Words

Transition words connect ideas between sentences and paragraphs. They guide the reader through your logic — without them, writing feels choppy and disconnected.

---

## Categories

### 1. Addition (Adding More)

| Transition | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **and** | I like tea **and** coffee. |
| **also** | She's smart. She's **also** kind. |
| **in addition** | **In addition**, we offer free shipping. |
| **furthermore** | The plan is risky. **Furthermore**, it's expensive. |
| **moreover** | It's late. **Moreover**, I'm tired. |
| **besides** | I don't want to go. **Besides**, I have work. |
| **not only...but also** | She **not only** sings **but also** dances. |

### 2. Contrast (Showing Difference)

| Transition | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **but** | I called, **but** nobody answered. |
| **however** | It's expensive. **However**, the quality is excellent. |
| **on the other hand** | Cars are convenient. **On the other hand**, they pollute. |
| **although / even though** | **Although** I was tired, I kept working. |
| **nevertheless** | The test was hard. **Nevertheless**, I passed. |
| **whereas / while** | She likes coffee, **whereas** I prefer tea. |
| **in contrast** | The old system was slow. **In contrast**, the new one is fast. |
| **despite / in spite of** | **Despite** the rain, we went out. (+ noun/Ving) |

### 3. Cause & Effect

| Transition | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **so** | It rained, **so** we stayed home. |
| **because / since / as** | We stayed home **because** it rained. |
| **therefore** | The data is incomplete. **Therefore**, we can't conclude. |
| **as a result** | He didn't study. **As a result**, he failed. |
| **consequently** | The server crashed. **Consequently**, all users were affected. |
| **thus** (formal) | The experiment failed. **Thus**, we need a new approach. |
| **hence** (formal) | Costs have risen. **Hence** the price increase. |

### 4. Sequence / Time

| Transition | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **first / firstly** | **First**, preheat the oven. |
| **then / next / after that** | Mix the flour. **Then** add the eggs. |
| **finally / lastly** | **Finally**, bake for 30 minutes. |
| **meanwhile** | I was working. **Meanwhile**, she was sleeping. |
| **subsequently** | He was arrested and **subsequently** convicted. |
| **eventually** | We waited for hours. **Eventually**, he showed up. |

### 5. Examples & Emphasis

| Transition | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **for example** | Many fruits are healthy — **for example**, apples and bananas. |
| **for instance** | Some countries, **for instance** Japan, have aging populations. |
| **such as** | Citrus fruits **such as** oranges and lemons... |
| **in fact** | He's not just smart. **In fact**, he's a genius. |
| **indeed** | The problem is serious. **Indeed**, it's critical. |
| **especially** | I love desserts, **especially** chocolate cake. |

### 6. Conclusion / Summary

| Transition | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **in conclusion** | **In conclusion**, the evidence is clear. |
| **to summarize** | **To summarize**, we have three options. |
| **overall** | **Overall**, it was a good year. |
| **in short** | **In short**, we need more time. |
| **all in all** | **All in all**, I'm satisfied with the result. |

---

## Punctuation Rules

| Position | Rule | Example |
|----------|------|---------|
| **Start of sentence** | Transition + comma | **However**, I disagree. |
| **Middle of sentence** | Comma before + comma after | The plan is risky, **however**, and needs review. |
| **Between clauses** | Semicolon + transition + comma | I was tired**; however,** I kept working. |

> ✅ "I was tired. **However**, I kept working."
> ✅ "I was tired**; however,** I kept working."
> ❌ "I was tired, however, I kept working." (comma splice)

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai often omits explicit transitions because context carries the logic. English requires them.

| Thai Style | ❌ | ✅ |
|-----------|------|-----|
| Implicit contrast | It rained. We went out. | It rained. **However**, we went out. |
| Implicit cause | He didn't study. He failed. | He didn't study. **As a result**, he failed. |
| And... and... and... | I woke up and ate and went to work. | I woke up. **Then** I ate. **After that**, I went to work. |
