# Conjunctions

Conjunctions connect words, phrases, and clauses. They're the glue that turns short, choppy sentences into smooth, natural English.

---

## Three Types of Conjunctions

| Type | Job | Examples |
|------|-----|----------|
| **Coordinating** | Join equal elements | FANBOYS |
| **Subordinating** | Join dependent clause to main clause | because, although, if, when |
| **Correlative** | Paired conjunctions | either...or, neither...nor |

---

## 1. Coordinating Conjunctions — FANBOYS

Join two equal (same-level) elements: word+word, phrase+phrase, clause+clause.

| Conjunction | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| **F**or | Because (formal) | I left, **for** I was tired. |
| **A**nd | Addition | I like tea **and** coffee. |
| **N**or | Negative addition | He doesn't smoke, **nor** does he drink. |
| **B**ut | Contrast | She's small **but** strong. |
| **O**r | Alternative | Tea **or** coffee? |
| **Y**et | Surprising contrast | He's rich, **yet** he's unhappy. |
| **S**o | Result / Consequence | I was hungry, **so** I ate. |

### Punctuation with FANBOYS

| Joining... | Comma before FANBOYS? | Example |
|-----------|:--------------------:|---------|
| Two words | ❌ No | tea **and** coffee |
| Two phrases | ❌ No | in the morning **or** in the evening |
| Two independent clauses | ✅ Yes | I wanted to go, **but** I was too tired. |

---

## 2. Subordinating Conjunctions

Introduce dependent clauses — they make one clause subordinate (dependent) to another.

| Category | Conjunction | Example |
|----------|------------|---------|
| **Time** | when, while, after, before, until, as soon as, since | **When** I arrived, she left. |
| **Reason** | because, since, as | I stayed home **because** it was raining. |
| **Condition** | if, unless, provided that | **If** you study, you'll pass. |
| **Contrast** | although, even though, whereas, while | **Although** it rained, we went out. |
| **Purpose** | so that, in order that | I left early **so that** I could catch the bus. |
| **Comparison** | than, as...as | She's taller **than** I am. |

### Punctuation Rule

> Dependent clause FIRST → comma. Dependent clause SECOND → no comma.
> "**Because** it was raining, I stayed home." vs "I stayed home **because** it was raining."

---

## 3. Correlative Conjunctions

Paired conjunctions that link balanced elements.

| Pair | Example |
|------|---------|
| **both...and** | She is **both** smart **and** kind. |
| **either...or** | We can go **either** today **or** tomorrow. |
| **neither...nor** | He is **neither** rich **nor** famous. |
| **not only...but also** | She **not only** sings **but also** dances. |
| **whether...or** | I don't know **whether** to stay **or** go. |

⚠️ **Parallel structure matters:** "She is **not only** a great singer **but also** a talented dancer." (both nouns with adjectives)

---

## Common Conjunction Errors

| Error | ❌ | ✅ |
|-------|---|---|
| Starting sentence with "And" or "But" | (Acceptable in informal writing, avoid in formal) | **However**, / **In addition**, |
| "Although...but" (double contrast) | **Although** it rained, **but** we went. | **Although** it rained, we went. / It rained, **but** we went. |
| "Because...so" (double reason-result) | **Because** I was tired, **so** I left. | **Because** I was tired, I left. / I was tired, **so** I left. |

> English uses **ONE conjunction per clause relationship**, not pairs like Thai.

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai often uses paired connectors — `เพราะว่า...ก็เลย` (because...so) and `ถึงแม้ว่า...แต่ก็` (although...but). English forbids this.

| Thai Pattern | ❌ | ✅ |
|-------------|------|-----|
| เพราะว่าฝนตก ฉันก็เลยอยู่บ้าน | Because it rained, so I stayed home. | **Because** it rained, I stayed home. |
| ถึงแม้ว่าเขารวย แต่เขาก็ไม่มีความสุข | Although he is rich, but he is not happy. | **Although** he is rich, he is not happy. |
