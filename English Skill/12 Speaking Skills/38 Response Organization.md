# Response Organization

Both IELTS and TOEFL speaking sections test how well you **organize** your ideas — not just whether you can speak English. A structured response scores higher than a rambling one, even with simpler vocabulary.

---

## IELTS Speaking Structure

IELTS Speaking has 3 parts (11-14 minutes total).

### Part 1: Introduction & Interview (4-5 min)

Simple questions about familiar topics. Answer + give a reason.

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| **Answer + Reason** | "Yes, I enjoy reading. **Because** it helps me relax after work." |
| **Answer + Example** | "I usually cook at home. **For example**, I made pad thai last night." |
| **Answer + Detail** | "I live in Bangkok. **It's** a busy city with great food." |

> **Length:** 2-3 sentences. Don't ramble.

### Part 2: Long Turn / Cue Card (3-4 min)

You get a card with a topic and 3-4 bullet points. You speak for **1-2 minutes**.

#### The CUE CARD Template

```
Describe [topic]
You should say:
- what/who it is
- when/where you experienced it
- why it was important
- and explain how you felt about it
```

#### Response Structure

| Section | Time | What to Say |
|---------|------|-------------|
| **Opening** | 5 sec | "I'd like to talk about..." |
| **Point 1** | 20 sec | Answer the first bullet |
| **Point 2** | 20 sec | Answer the second bullet |
| **Point 3** | 20 sec | Answer the third bullet |
| **Explanation** | 20 sec | The "explain" bullet (most important) |
| **Closing** | 10 sec | "So that's why..." / "Overall..." |

#### Example Cue Card

> Describe a book that influenced you.
> - what book it is
> - when you read it
> - what it is about
> - and explain why it influenced you

**Model Response (1.5 min):**

> "I'd like to talk about a book called 'Atomic Habits' by James Clear. I first read it about two years ago when I was struggling with my study routine.
>
> The book is about how small, consistent changes can lead to big results over time. It explains the science behind habit formation and gives practical strategies for building good habits and breaking bad ones.
>
> What I found most useful was the idea of 'habit stacking' — linking a new habit to an existing one. For example, I started studying English right after my morning coffee, and now it's automatic.
>
> This book influenced me because it changed how I think about self-improvement. Instead of trying to make big changes overnight, I now focus on getting 1% better every day. So overall, it's had a really positive impact on my life."

### Part 3: Discussion (4-5 min)

Abstract questions related to Part 2's topic. Longer, more complex answers.

#### Response Structure for Part 3

| Step | What to Say |
|------|-------------|
| **Opinion** | "I think... / I believe... / In my view..." |
| **Reason** | "This is because... / The main reason is..." |
| **Example** | "For instance... / For example..." |
| **Alternative** (optional) | "However, some people might argue that..." |
| **Conclusion** | "So overall... / That's why I think..." |

## TOEFL Speaking Structure

TOEFL Speaking has 4 tasks (17 minutes total).

### Independent Task (Task 1) — 45 seconds

Express and support a personal opinion.

| Time | What to Say |
|------|-------------|
| **0-5 sec** | State your opinion clearly |
| **5-20 sec** | First reason + example |
| **20-35 sec** | Second reason + example |
| **35-45 sec** | Quick conclusion |

#### Template

> "I believe that [opinion]. **First**, [reason 1]. **For example**, [specific example]. **Second**, [reason 2]. **For instance**, [specific example]. **That's why** I think [restate opinion]."

### Integrated Tasks (Tasks 2-4) — 60 seconds

Summarize what you read and/or heard.

| Task | Input | Template |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Task 2** | Read a passage + listen to a conversation | "The [announcement/proposal] says [main point]. The student [agrees/disagrees] because [reason 1] and [reason 2]." |
| **Task 3** | Read a passage + listen to a lecture | "The passage explains [concept]. The professor illustrates this with [example]. [Describe example in detail]." |
| **Task 4** | Listen to a lecture only | "The professor discusses [topic]. [Example 1]. [Example 2]." |

## Speaking Speed & Timing

| IELTS | TOEFL |
|-------|-------|
| Speak naturally — no timer visible | 45-60 seconds — tight! |
| Aim for 1.5-2 min in Part 2 | Practice with a timer |
| Part 3: 4-6 sentences per answer | Don't run out of things to say |

### How to Practice Timing

1. Record yourself with your phone
2. Set a timer for 45 seconds (TOEFL) or 2 minutes (IELTS Part 2)
3. Speak until the timer ends
4. Listen back — did you finish your points? Did you leave long pauses?

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| Trap | Problem | Fix |
|------|---------|-----|
| One-word answers | "Yes." / "No." | Always add a reason: "Yes, because..." |
| Long pauses ("uhh...") | Shows lack of fluency | Use fillers: "Well...", "Let me think...", "That's an interesting question" |
| Going off-topic | Rambling about unrelated things | Follow the bullet points (IELTS) or the template (TOEFL) |
| Speaking too quietly | Examiner can't hear you | Practice projecting your voice |
| Memorizing scripts | Examiners can tell — you lose marks | Practice templates, not scripts. Be flexible. |
| Not concluding | Just stopping mid-thought | Always add: "So that's why..." / "Overall..." |
