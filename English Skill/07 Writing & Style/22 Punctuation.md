# Punctuation

Punctuation marks are the traffic signals of writing — they tell the reader when to pause, stop, or pay attention. Wrong punctuation can completely change meaning.

---

## The Essential Marks

| Mark | Symbol | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| Period / Full stop | `.` | End a statement |
| Comma | `,` | Separate, pause, list |
| Semicolon | `;` | Join related independent clauses |
| Colon | `:` | Introduce a list, explanation, or quote |
| Apostrophe | `'` | Possession, contraction |
| Question mark | `?` | End a question |
| Exclamation mark | `!` | Strong emotion |
| Quotation marks | `" "` or `' '` | Direct speech, titles |
| Hyphen | `-` | Join words (compound adjectives) |
| Dash | `—` | Parenthetical break — like this |
| Parentheses | `( )` | Extra information |

---

## 1. Period (Full Stop)

- End declarative sentences: "She went home."
- Abbreviations: "Mr.", "Dr.", "etc.", "e.g."
- **One space** after a period (not two — that's a typewriter-era habit).

## 2. Comma — The Most Misused Mark

### Use Commas For:

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Lists** (Oxford comma optional) | I bought apples, bananas**,** and oranges. |
| **After introductory phrases** | **After the meeting,** we went for lunch. |
| **Before FANBOYS joining independent clauses** | I wanted to go**,** but I was too tired. |
| **Non-essential (extra) information** | My brother**,** who lives in London**,** is a doctor. |
| **Direct address** | John**,** can you help me? |
| **Tag questions** | You're coming**,** aren't you? |

### DON'T Use Commas:

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| I like coffee, and tea. | I like coffee and tea. | Not two independent clauses |
| She said, that she was tired. | She said that she was tired. | No comma before "that" in reported speech |
| The man, who called, was my boss. | The man who called was my boss. | Essential info — no commas |

### The Oxford Comma Debate

| With Oxford (Serial) Comma | Without |
|---------------------------|---------|
| I love my parents, Lady Gaga, and Humpty Dumpty. | I love my parents, Lady Gaga and Humpty Dumpty. |

> Without the Oxford comma, "Lady Gaga and Humpty Dumpty" could be read as your parents! The Oxford comma prevents ambiguity.

## 3. Semicolon

**Use:** Join two closely related independent clauses WITHOUT a conjunction.

| ✅ | ❌ |
|---|-----|
| I have a big test tomorrow**; I** can't go out tonight. | I have a big test tomorrow, I can't go out tonight. (comma splice) |

Also: separate complex list items that contain commas:
> I've lived in Bangkok, Thailand**;** Tokyo, Japan**;** and Seoul, Korea.

## 4. Colon — "Pay Attention, Here It Comes"

Use before a list, explanation, or quote — but only after a complete sentence.

| ✅ | ❌ |
|---|-----|
| Bring three things**:** a pen, paper, and ID. | Bring**:** a pen, paper, and ID. (fragment before colon) |
| She had one goal**:** to win. | Her goal was**:** to win. |

## 5. Apostrophe

### Possession
| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Singular noun | the **dog's** bone, **James's** car |
| Plural noun ending in s | the **dogs'** bones, the **students'** books |
| Plural noun not ending in s | the **children's** toys |

### Contractions
| Full | Contraction |
|------|------------|
| it is / it has | **it's** |
| do not | **don't** |
| cannot | **can't** |
| will not | **won't** |

### ⚠️ Its vs It's

| Form | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **its** | Possession (belongs to it) | The dog wagged **its** tail. |
| **it's** | Contraction (it is / it has) | **It's** raining. |

> If you can replace it with "it is" or "it has" → use **it's**. Otherwise → **its**.

## 6. Quotation Marks

| Style | Example |
|-------|---------|
| **Double** (US standard) | She said, "I'm tired." |
| **Single** (UK standard; US for quotes-inside-quotes) | She said, 'He told me, "I'm leaving."' |

**Period/comma INSIDE quotes** (US): She said, "I'm tired**.**"
**Period/comma OUTSIDE quotes** (UK): She said, 'I'm tired'**.**

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai writing has minimal punctuation — spaces separate clauses where English uses commas and periods.

| Thai Pattern | ❌ English | ✅ English |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|
| No comma in lists | I like apples bananas and oranges. | I like apples**,** bananas**,** and oranges. |
| Space between clauses | I was tired I went to bed. | I was tired**, so** I went to bed. |
| No apostrophe | My brothers car. | My **brother's** car. |
