# Parallel Structure

Parallel structure means keeping the same grammatical form in a list, comparison, or series. When items match in form, sentences flow smoothly. When they don't, the reader stumbles.

---

## The Core Rule

> Items in a series must share the same grammatical form.

| ❌ Not Parallel | ✅ Parallel |
|----------------|------------|
| I like **swimming**, **to run**, and **biking**. | I like **swimming**, **running**, and **biking**. |
| She is **smart**, **kind**, and **has patience**. | She is **smart**, **kind**, and **patient**. |

---

## Common Parallel Structures

### 1. Lists of Verbs

| ❌ | ✅ |
|---|-----|
| He **woke up**, **ate** breakfast, and **was going** to work. | He **woke up**, **ate** breakfast, and **went** to work. |

### 2. Lists of Nouns

| ❌ | ✅ |
|---|-----|
| I need **a pen**, **paper**, and **something to write on**. | I need **a pen**, **paper**, and **a notebook**. |

### 3. Lists of Adjectives

| ❌ | ✅ |
|---|-----|
| The movie was **exciting**, **funny**, and **it made me think**. | The movie was **exciting**, **funny**, and **thought-provoking**. |

### 4. Lists of Gerunds (-ing)

| ❌ | ✅ |
|---|-----|
| I enjoy **reading**, **to cook**, and **travel**. | I enjoy **reading**, **cooking**, and **traveling**. |

### 5. Lists of Infinitives (to V1)

| ❌ | ✅ |
|---|-----|
| I want **to graduate**, **get a job**, and **traveling**. | I want **to graduate**, **get a job**, and **travel**. |

---

## Paired Conjunctions (Correlatives)

With `both...and`, `either...or`, `neither...nor`, `not only...but also` — what follows each part must be parallel.

| ❌ | ✅ |
|---|-----|
| She is **not only** smart **but also** she works hard. | She is **not only** smart **but also** hard-working. |
| We can go **either** by car **or** taking the bus. | We can go **either** by car **or** by bus. |
| He **neither** called **nor** he texted. | He **neither** called **nor** texted. |

---

## Comparisons

Words like `than` and `as...as` need parallel elements.

| ❌ | ✅ |
|---|-----|
| Driving is faster **than** to walk. | **Driving** is faster **than** **walking**. |
| Her English is better **than** me. | Her English is better **than** **mine**. (not "than me" — comparing English to English, not English to me) |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai grammar doesn't require parallel forms — verbs don't conjugate, nouns don't have gerund/infinitive distinctions.

| ❌ | ✅ |
|---|-----|
| I like swim, to run, and biking. | I like **swimming**, **running**, and **biking**. |
| She is beautiful, smart, and has kindness. | She is **beautiful**, **smart**, and **kind**. |

---

## Quick Test

Fix the parallelism:

1. He enjoys hiking, to swim, and biking. → **hiking, swimming, and biking** ✅
2. The job requires attention to detail, being punctual, and you must work hard. → **attention to detail, punctuality, and hard work** ✅
3. She not only sings but also dancing. → **She not only sings but also dances.** ✅
