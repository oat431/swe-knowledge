# Capitalization Rules

English capitalizes far more words than Thai. Getting capitalization wrong in formal writing looks careless — but the rules are systematic.

---

## What MUST Be Capitalized

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| **First word of a sentence** | **T**he meeting starts at 9. |
| **The pronoun "I"** | **I** think **I** can do it. |
| **Proper nouns** (specific names) | **B**angkok, **J**ohn, **T**oyota, **G**oogle |
| **Days of the week** | **M**onday, **T**uesday |
| **Months** | **J**anuary, **F**ebruary |
| **Holidays** | **C**hristmas, **S**ongkran, **N**ew Year's Day |
| **Countries, nationalities, languages** | **T**hailand, **T**hai, **E**nglish |
| **Titles (before names)** | **P**resident Obama, **D**r. Smith, **P**rofessor Lee |
| **Titles of works** | *The **L**ord of the **R**ings*, ***H**arry **P**otter* |

---

## Title Capitalization

For titles of books, movies, articles — capitalize major words:

| Capitalize | Don't Capitalize (unless first/last) |
|-----------|-------------------------------------|
| Nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs | Articles (a, an, the) |
| First and last word (always) | Short prepositions (in, on, at, for) |
| | Coordinating conjunctions (and, but, or) |

> *The **C**atcher **i**n the **R**ye* ✅
> ***G**one **w**ith the **W**ind* ✅

---

## What is NOT Capitalized

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Seasons** (generally) | spring, summer (but: "Summer Olympics" as a title) |
| **General nouns** | I went to **u**niversity. (but: "Chulalongkorn **U**niversity") |
| **Directions** (as directions) | Drive **s**outh on the highway. (but: "the **S**outh" = region) |
| **After a colon** (usually) | One rule: always be on time. (US style sometimes capitalizes) |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai has no capitalization — this is a completely foreign concept.

| ❌ Common Error | ✅ Correct |
|----------------|-----------|
| i am from thailand. | **I** am from **T**hailand. |
| My name is john. | My name is **J**ohn. |
| I speak english and thai. | I speak **E**nglish and **T**hai. |
| I was born on monday, january 5th. | I was born on **M**onday, **J**anuary 5th. |

> **Rule of thumb:** If it's a specific name of something, it probably needs a capital letter.
