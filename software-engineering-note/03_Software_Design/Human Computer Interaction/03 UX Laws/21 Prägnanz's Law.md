# 21 Prägnanz's Law (Law of Simplicity)

> **People perceive and interpret ambiguous or complex images in the simplest form possible.**

The brain reduces complexity automatically. In UI, this means: if your design can be interpreted simply, it will be. If it requires effort to parse, users will struggle or misinterpret it.

---

## The Principle

![Prägnanz - brain simplifies complex shapes](https://lawsofux.com/images/pragnanz.svg)

*Source: lawsofux.com*

The Olympic rings are seen as 5 overlapping circles — not as a collection of 9 curved line segments. The brain picks the simplest interpretation.

---

## In UI Design

### Icons

| ❌ Overly Complex | ✅ Simple (Prägnanz-friendly) |
|------------------|------------------------------|
| A detailed home icon with roof tiles, chimney, door, windows | Simple house shape — a triangle on a rectangle |
| Settings icon with 10 gear teeth | Simple gear or 3 sliders |

### Data Display

| ❌ | ✅ |
|----|-----|
| Raw numbers: 1,247,893 | "1.2M" — brain simplifies |
| Long decimal: 0.873214 | "87%" — simpler perception |
| Complex table with 12 columns | Highlighted key metrics with expandable details |

### Layout

| ❌ | ✅ |
|----|-----|
| Asymmetrical, chaotic layout | Grid-based, aligned layout — brain sees order |
| Multiple competing visual styles | Consistent design language — brain recognizes the pattern |

---

## Practical Rules

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Use symmetry and alignment** | Center-aligned or grid-aligned elements feel simpler |
| **Group related items** | Card or border around related info — brain sees one unit |
| **Remove unnecessary detail** | Simplify icons, truncate numbers, collapse secondary info |
| **Consistent patterns** | Same button style everywhere = brain learns once |
