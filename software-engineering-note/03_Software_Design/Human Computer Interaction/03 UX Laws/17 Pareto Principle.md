# 17 Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule)

> **80% of effects come from 20% of causes.**

In UX: 80% of your users use 20% of your features. Focus your design effort on that 20% — the features people actually use.

---

## The Principle

![Pareto Principle - 80/20 distribution](https://lawsofux.com/images/pareto-principle.svg)

*Source: lawsofux.com*

---

## What This Means for UX

| Area | 20% (Focus Here) | 80% (Don't Over-Invest) |
|------|-----------------|------------------------|
| **Features** | Core flows (login, search, checkout) | Edge cases, power-user features |
| **Users** | Primary user persona | Rare edge-case users |
| **Bugs** | 20% of bugs cause 80% of crashes | Minor cosmetic issues |
| **Content** | 20% of pages get 80% of traffic | Deep documentation, old blog posts |
| **Design effort** | Polish the screens users see daily | Settings pages seen once per year |

---

## How to Apply It

### 1. Find Your 20%

| Method | Tool |
|--------|------|
| Analytics | Which pages/screens get the most traffic? |
| Feature usage | Which features do users actually click? |
| Support tickets | What are the top 3 complaints? |
| User testing | Where do users spend the most time? |

### 2. Invest Disproportionately

> A 10% improvement to a feature used by 80% of users is worth more than a 100% improvement to a feature used by 2% of users.

---

## The Danger

Don't use Pareto as an excuse to ignore the 80% entirely. Some of that 80% is critical (error handling, accessibility, security). The principle is about **effort allocation**, not abandonment.
