# 14 Teamwork

Software is built by teams. The ethics of teamwork govern how we treat each other, how we estimate, and how we hold ourselves accountable.

---

## Work as a Team

> You are not a collection of individuals assigned to tasks. You are a team responsible for an outcome.

| Team | Group of Individuals |
|------|---------------------|
| "We failed." | "Bob failed." |
| "Let me help." | "That's not my problem." |
| Knowledge shared | Knowledge hoarded |
| Collective ownership | Individual code ownership |

---

## Open / Virtual Office

Co-location improves communication and serendipity. But remote work is reality for many teams. The key: **fight the distance.**

| Co-Located | Remote |
|-----------|--------|
| Whiteboard conversations | Video calls with screen share |
| Tap on the shoulder | Instant message |
| Lunch together | Virtual coffee breaks |
| Serendipity happens naturally | Serendipity requires deliberate effort |

## Estimate Honestly and Fairly

### The Three Components of Estimation

| Component | What It Means |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Honesty** | Tell the truth. Don't pad. Don't lowball. Don't tell them what they want to hear. |
| **Accuracy** | Close to the actual effort. Not "I think maybe 2 weeks" based on nothing. |
| **Precision** | The right level of detail. A single number ("10 days") implies false precision. A range ("5–15 days") is more honest. |

### Two Stories About Lying

**Story 1: Vectors.** Developer estimates 3 days for a vector math library. Manager says "make it 2." Developer complies. Takes 4 days. Manager learns to distrust estimates. Developer learns to pad estimates. Everyone loses.

**Story 2: pCCU.** Developer estimates "6–8 weeks." Manager demands "3 weeks." Developer says "I can't." Manager escalates. VP asks developer directly. Developer stands firm. VP respects the honesty. Project takes 7 weeks.

### The Lesson

> When you lie about estimates, you destroy trust. When you stand firm on honest estimates, you build trust — even if the news isn't what they want to hear.

---

## Accuracy and Precision

```
Accurate + Precise   = Bullseye. The best.
Accurate + Imprecise = "5–15 days" when it takes 10. Honest and useful.
Inaccurate + Precise = "Exactly 8 days" when it takes 15. The worst kind of lie.
Inaccurate + Imprecise = "Somewhere between 3 days and 6 months." Useless.
```

> Aim for accurate, even if you must be imprecise. A range is better than a precise lie.
