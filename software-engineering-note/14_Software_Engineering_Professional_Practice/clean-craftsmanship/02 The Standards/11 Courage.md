# 11 Courage

The standards of productivity and quality require courage. It takes guts to say no, to give honest estimates, and to keep learning when it's easier to coast.

---

## We Cover for Each Other

> A team is not a collection of individuals. It's a unit. When one falls, the others catch them.

| Team Behavior | Not Team Behavior |
|--------------|-----------------|
| "I'll help you with that." | "That's not my code." |
| Knowledge shared freely | Knowledge hoarded |
| Bus factor > 2 for every module | Only Bob knows how that works |
| Sick colleague? Work redistributed. | Sick colleague? Project blocked. |

---

## Honest Estimates

> The most courageous thing a developer can do is give an honest estimate.

### What Honesty Looks Like

| Situation | Honest Response |
|-----------|----------------|
| Asked for an estimate on unfamiliar work | "I don't know yet. Let me spike it for a day and get back to you." |
| Asked to commit to an exact date | "Based on current knowledge, between 5 and 15 days." |
| Asked if it can be done faster | "Yes, by reducing scope. Here's what we could cut." |
| Realize an estimate was wrong | Tell them immediately. Don't wait. |

### Lies Developers Tell Themselves

| Lie | Truth |
|-----|-------|
| "I'll make up the time later." | You won't. Velocity data proves this. |
| "It's almost done — just a few more days." | "Almost done" is 90% done with 90% remaining. |
| "If I work weekends, I can hit the date." | You'll burn out, the code will be worse, and you'll still miss the date. |

## You Must Say NO

> Saying no is not insubordination. It's the professional's primary obligation.

When you say yes to an impossible deadline, you're not being a team player. You're lying. The truth will come out — but only after weeks of overtime, broken promises, and damaged trust.

**Say no early.** It's uncomfortable for 5 minutes. Saying yes and failing is painful for months.

---

## Continuous Aggressive Learning

> The software industry changes faster than any other. If you're not learning aggressively, you're falling behind.

| Minimum | Aggressive |
|---------|-----------|
| 20 hours/week of professional development | Every spare moment |
| Read blogs, books | Write blogs, books |
| Attend conferences | Speak at conferences |
| Do your job | Teach others your job |

---

## Mentoring

> Teaching is the best way to learn. Every senior should mentor juniors. Every junior should seek mentors.

Mentoring is not a favor. It's a professional obligation. The knowledge in your head is worth nothing if it dies with you. Pass it on.
