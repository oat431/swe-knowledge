# Clean Craftsmanship — Overview

> *Source: Clean Craftsmanship: Disciplines, Standards, and Ethics by Robert C. Martin (2022, Addison-Wesley)*

The third book in Uncle Bob's "Clean" trilogy. *Clean Code* covered the craft. *Clean Coder* covered the professional. *Clean Craftsmanship* covers the **disciplines, standards, and ethics** that define a true software craftsman.

---

## Structure — Three Parts

| Part | Chapters | Focus |
|------|----------|-------|
| **I: The Disciplines** | 1–8 | TDD, Refactoring, Simple Design, Collaborative Programming, Acceptance Tests |
| **II: The Standards** | 9–11 | Productivity, Quality, Courage — your new CTO's expectations |
| **III: The Ethics** | 12–14 | Harm, Integrity, Teamwork — the programmer's oath |

## Core Philosophy

- **Craftsmanship = disciplines + standards + ethics.** You can't have one without the others.
- **The disciplines are non-negotiable.** TDD, refactoring, simple design, pairing, acceptance tests. Without them, you're not a craftsman.
- **Standards are your CTO's expectations.** If your imaginary CTO wouldn't accept it, don't ship it.
- **Ethics are the programmer's oath.** First, do no harm. To function. To structure. Continuously.
