# Clean Agile — Overview

> *Source: Clean Agile: Back to Basics by Robert C. Martin (2020, Prentice Hall)*
> *Book summary by Patrick Bucher (2023)*

A return to the core of Agile — not the bloated, consultant-driven frameworks, but the small, disciplined practices that actually work. Uncle Bob argues that Agile has been hijacked by process peddlers and that the original values and technical practices are what matter.

## Core Philosophy

- **Agile is a small discipline for small teams.** Big projects are made of small projects.
- **The only way to go fast is to go well.** Cutting quality slows you down.
- **Technical practices are non-negotiable.** Without TDD, refactoring, and simple design, Agile becomes a mess-making machine.
- **Data replaces hope.** Velocity and burn-down charts give honest visibility.
- **Professionalism = discipline.** Not ceremony. Not process. Actual daily practice.
