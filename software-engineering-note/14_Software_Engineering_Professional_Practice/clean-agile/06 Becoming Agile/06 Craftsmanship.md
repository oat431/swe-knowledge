# 06 Craftsmanship (by Sandro Mancuso)

Agile was quickly adopted — and quickly distorted. It became a process to deliver software faster, not better. Developers were micro-managed again, with the transparency Agile provided used **against** them. The Software Craftsmanship movement was the response.

---

## The Agile Hangover

| Agile's Promise | What Actually Happened |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Heal the business-development divide | Divide came back stronger |
| Developer autonomy | Managers defined roadmaps and milestones |
| Sustainable pace | One top-priority story after another in a hurry |
| Technical excellence | Technical practices ignored |

> Agile training only covered business and team-facing practices — as if better communication alone would make developers more skilled. It takes technical skill to release software in short cycles while maintaining quality.

---

## The Software Craftsmanship Manifesto (2008)

Built on top of the Agile Manifesto. A developers' movement — and they don't want it hijacked again.

| Agile Manifesto | Software Craftsmanship Extension |
|----------------|--------------------------------|
| Working software | **Well-crafted** software |
| Responding to change | **Steadily adding value** |
| Individuals and interactions | A **community of professionals** |
| Customer collaboration | **Productive partnerships** |

> *"That is, in pursuit of the items on the left we have found the items on the right to be indispensable."*

---

## Ideologies vs Methodologies

| | Ideology | Methodology |
|---|----------|------------|
| **What it is** | System of ideas, ideals to aim at | System of methods and practices |
| **Agile** | Ideology — goal: business agility, customer satisfaction | |
| **Scrum, XP** | | Methodologies to reach those goals |
| **Software Craftsmanship** | Ideology — doesn't prescribe a fixed set of practices | Promotes perpetual search for better ones |

> Methodologies are like training wheels: useful to get started, but soon become a hindrance. Too much focus on methods distracts from goals. Still, practices matter — professionals are defined by the way they work.

---

## What Craftsmanship Values

| Value | What It Means |
|-------|--------------|
| **XP as best practice set** | Since 2008, the community considers XP to provide the best practices. |
| **Goals before practices** | When discussing practices, first agree on goals — then look at practices as ways to reach them. |
| **Programming as identity** | Craftspeople don't just work as programmers. They **are** programmers. It's part of their identity. |
| **Investment in career** | Not just a paycheck. Continuous learning. But this doesn't mean no private life beyond the profession. |

---

## Agile + Craftsmanship = The Complete Picture

> Agile and Software Craftsmanship share the same goals. Combined, they are the perfect way to reach them.

Agile provides the values and business practices. Craftsmanship adds the technical rigor and professional identity. You need both.
