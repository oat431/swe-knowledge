# PMBOK — Essential Documents by Phase

> **Source:** [[PMBOK v8 - Overview|PMBOK® Guide — Eighth Edition (PMI, 2025)]]
> Organized by the five Project Management Focus Areas: Initiating → Planning → Executing → Monitoring & Controlling → Closing
> Plus cross-cutting documents (Governance, Procurement, Stakeholders, Communications)
>
> ⚠️ **This is a document-only extract.** For the full body of knowledge (principles, processes, tools, techniques), see:
> `F:\projects\orlita_md\software-engineering-note\Body of Knowledge\PMBOK\`

**Priority Legend:**

| Icon | Level | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 🔴 | **Must Have** | Essential for virtually all projects |
| 🟡 | **Nice to Have** | Recommended for medium+ complexity |

## 1. Initiating

|---|---|---|---|

## Life Cycle Integration

Per [[03_Project_Life_Cycles]], these documents apply across all development approaches — predictive, adaptive, or hybrid. The **level of formality** depends on the approach:

| Approach | Documentation Style |
|---|---|
| **Predictive (Waterfall)** | Formal, comprehensive, signed-off baselines |
| **Adaptive (Agile)** | Lighter — user stories replace SRS, backlog replaces WBS, burn charts replace EVM |
| **Hybrid** | Formal for fixed-scope components; lighter for iterative components |

> **Tailoring Note:** Per [[11_Tailoring]], adjust which documents to produce, their level of detail, and review/approval rigor based on: project complexity, criticality, team size, organizational culture, regulatory requirements, and stakeholder expectations. *No project needs every document — select what adds value.*
