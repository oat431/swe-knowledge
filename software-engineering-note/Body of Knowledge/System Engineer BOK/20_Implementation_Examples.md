# 20 — Implementation Examples (Case Studies)

> *Source: SEBoK v2, Part 7 — Systems Engineering Implementation Examples*

## Purpose

This part presents real-world case studies that illustrate systems engineering principles in practice — both successes and failures. Each case demonstrates specific SE lessons learned and provides context for how SE methods are applied (or should have been applied) across diverse domains.

## Defense System Examples

- **Submarine Warfare Federated Tactical Systems** — Integration of multiple combat systems on naval platforms, highlighting the challenges of federated SoS architecture, interface management, and evolutionary capability insertion.
- **Virginia Class Submarine** — A model of SE-driven acquisition, demonstrating how early SE investment in requirements, modular design, and integrated product teams reduced cost and accelerated delivery compared to previous submarine programs.

## Information System Examples

- **Complex Adaptive Taxi Service Scheduler** — Applying complex adaptive systems principles to dynamic scheduling, illustrating emergent behavior, adaptation, and the limits of traditional deterministic approaches.
- **Successful Business Transformation within a Russian IT Company** — Organizational transformation through SE principles, showing how SE thinking applied to business processes and culture.
- **FBI Virtual Case File System** — A classic SE failure: poorly defined requirements, scope creep, inadequate architecture, and lack of stakeholder engagement led to a $170M system that was never deployed.

## Medical System Examples

- **Next Generation Medical Infusion Pump** — Applying SE to safety-critical medical device development, demonstrating hazard analysis, human factors engineering, and regulatory compliance integration.
- **Medical Radiation** — Lessons from radiation therapy incidents where SE failures (software errors, inadequate verification, poor human-system interface design) led to patient harm.
- **Design for Maintainability** — How SE principles enable system maintainability throughout the life cycle, using medical device examples.

## Space System Examples

- **Global Positioning System (GPS)** — SE at the scale of a global utility: constellation architecture, signal structure definition, ground control integration, and sustaining engineering across decades.
- **Cassini/Huygens Mission to Saturn** — International collaboration challenges when information sharing failures between NASA and ESA nearly compromised the mission.
- **Hubble Space Telescope** — The famous primary mirror flaw as an SE lesson: inadequate end-to-end system verification despite component-level testing.
- **Thirty-Meter Telescope** — Applying MBSE to requirements analysis for next-generation astronomical systems.
- **Apollo 1 Disaster** — The tragic consequences of inadequate systems safety engineering — a pure oxygen atmosphere, inward-opening hatch, and flammable materials.

## Transportation System Examples

- **Denver Airport Baggage Handling System** — Perhaps the most famous SE failure: a highly automated system where complexity outpaced SE capability, resulting in massive delays and cost overruns.
- **FAA Advanced Automation System (AAS)** — Requirements that grew beyond feasible implementation, demonstrating the danger of unconstrained stakeholder expectations without SE discipline.
- **FAA NextGen Air Transportation System** — Transforming a national airspace system through SoS engineering approaches.
- **UK West Coast Route Modernisation Project** — Successful application of SE to rail infrastructure modernization.

## Utilities Examples

- **Northwest Hydro System** — SE for complex water resource systems balancing power generation, flood control, environmental protection, and recreation.
- **Singapore Water Management** — An integrated SE approach to urban water sustainability covering supply diversification, demand management, and wastewater recycling.
