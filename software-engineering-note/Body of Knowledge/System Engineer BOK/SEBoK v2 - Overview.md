## What Is This?

The **Systems Engineering Body of Knowledge (SEBoK)** is the definitive reference for the systems engineering discipline. Originally developed by the BKCASE project (Body of Knowledge and Curriculum to Advance Systems Engineering), SEBoK is a living, wiki-based compendium that describes, organizes, and provides access to the generally accepted knowledge of systems engineering.

Version 2 represents a comprehensive update reflecting the evolution of SE practice — from traditional defense and aerospace applications through Model-Based Systems Engineering (MBSE), digital engineering, agile approaches, and emerging domains like healthcare, enterprise systems, and systems of systems. It is structured as eight parts progressing from foundations through applications to enabling and emerging knowledge.

SEBoK serves multiple audiences: SE novices, practicing systems engineers, engineers from other disciplines, customers, educators, researchers, and general managers. It is both a learning pathway and a working reference.

## Core Philosophy

> Systems engineering is an interdisciplinary approach and means to enable the realization of successful systems. It focuses on defining customer needs and required functionality early in the development cycle, documenting requirements, and then proceeding with design synthesis and system validation while considering the complete problem.

SEBoK emphasizes that systems engineering is not a single methodology but a **mindset and discipline** applicable across domains — from traditional defense systems to healthcare delivery, enterprise transformation, and socio-technical systems. The systems approach — identifying problems, synthesizing solutions, analyzing alternatives, implementing, and sustaining — is the common thread.
