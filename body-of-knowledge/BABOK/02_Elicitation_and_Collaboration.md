# Elicitation and Collaboration

> *Source: BABOK® Guide v3 by IIBA, Chapter 3 — Elicitation and Collaboration*

## Purpose

The Elicitation and Collaboration knowledge area describes the work of drawing out information from stakeholders and other sources, confirming that information for accuracy, communicating it to the right audiences, and managing ongoing stakeholder relationships throughout the business analysis effort. It comprises five tasks that span the full interaction lifecycle: preparing for engagement, conducting the actual elicitation, verifying results, sharing information, and sustaining productive collaboration.

## BACCM Core Concepts in Elicitation and Collaboration

| Core Concept | Application |
|---|---|
| **Change** | Determine characteristics of the change and stakeholder concerns; the change itself shapes what elicitation is needed |
| **Need** | Elicit, confirm, and communicate needs iteratively; understanding of needs evolves over time |
| **Solution** | Elicit, confirm, and communicate desired characteristics of proposed solutions |
| **Stakeholder** | Manage collaboration with all who participate; they engage in different roles at different times |
| **Value** | Collaborate with stakeholders to assess the relative value of elicited information and use techniques to confirm and communicate that value |
| **Context** | Apply elicitation techniques to identify business analysis information about circumstances affecting the change |

## 4.1 Prepare for Elicitation

### Purpose

Understand the scope of the elicitation activity, select appropriate techniques, and plan for (or procure) supporting materials and resources.

### Description

Preparation involves defining desired outcomes, considering stakeholders and initiative goals, determining which work products will result, selecting techniques best suited to produce those results, establishing logistics, identifying supporting materials, and fostering conditions for collaboration during elicitation activities.

### Inputs

| Input | Source |
|---|---|
| **Needs** | Guides scope and purpose of elicitation; some need must exist even if not fully understood |
| **Stakeholder Engagement Approach** | Understanding stakeholders' communication and collaboration needs shapes effective elicitation events |

### Elements

1. **Understand the Scope of Elicitation** — Consider business domain, corporate culture, stakeholder locations, group dynamics, expected outputs, practitioner skills, complementary activities, strategy/solution approach, future solution scope, and possible information sources. Understanding scope allows the BA to recognize when the activity strays, when resources are unavailable, and when elicitation is complete.

2. **Select Elicitation Techniques** — Multiple techniques are typically used per activity. Selection depends on cost/time constraints, information source types and access, organizational culture, desired outcomes, stakeholder needs, availability, and location (co-located vs. dispersed). The BA must be prepared to adjust selections as dynamics change.

3. **Set Up Logistics** — Plan activity goals, participants and roles, scheduled resources (people, rooms, tools), locations, communication channels, techniques, and stakeholder languages. May include creating an agenda.

4. **Secure Supporting Material** — Identify and procure needed information sources: people, systems, historical data, documents (existing system docs, business rules, policies, regulations, contracts), and draft analysis models. Additional planning may be needed for experimental elicitation with novel tools or equipment.

5. **Prepare Stakeholders** — Educate stakeholders on how techniques work, what information is needed, and the purpose of the activity. Provide agendas and supporting materials in advance. Ensure buy-in from all necessary stakeholders to prevent resistance. Research/exploration may be solo activities requiring no stakeholder preparation.

### Guidelines and Tools

| Guideline/Tool | Role |
|---|---|
| **Business Analysis Approach** | General strategy for BA work: methodology, stakeholder types and involvement, timing, expected format/detail of results, challenges |
| **Business Objectives** | Desired direction for achieving the future state; shapes elicitation events and supporting materials |
| **Existing Business Analysis Information** | Provides understanding of elicitation goals and aids preparation |
| **Potential Value** | Describes value from the proposed future state; shapes elicitation events |

### Techniques

- **Brainstorming** — Identify and reach consensus on information sources and most effective elicitation techniques
- **Data Mining** — Identify information or patterns requiring further investigation
- **Document Analysis** — Identify and assess candidate sources of supporting materials
- **Estimation** — Estimate time, effort, and cost for elicitation
- **Interviews** — Identify concerns about planned elicitation; seek authority to proceed
- **Mind Mapping** — Collaboratively identify and reach consensus on sources and techniques
- **Risk Analysis and Management** — Identify, assess, and manage conditions that could disrupt elicitation or affect quality/validity of results
- **Stakeholder List, Map, or Personas** — Determine who should be consulted, participate, and in what roles

### Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Role |
|---|---|
| **Domain Subject Matter Expert** | Provides supporting materials and guidance on information sources; helps arrange research and facilitated elicitation |
| **Project Manager** | Ensures appropriate people and resources are available |
| **Sponsor** | Approves or denies planned elicitation; authorizes and requires stakeholder participation |

### Outputs

- **Elicitation Activity Plan** — Includes logistics, scope, selected techniques, and supporting materials for each elicitation activity.

## 4.2 Conduct Elicitation

### Purpose

Draw out, explore, and identify information relevant to the change.

### Description

Three common types of elicitation:

- **Collaborative** — Direct interaction with stakeholders, relying on their experiences, expertise, and judgment.
- **Research** — Systematically discovering and studying information from materials or sources not directly known by stakeholders. Includes data analysis of historical data to identify trends or past results.
- **Experiments** — Identifying information that cannot be known without controlled tests (observational studies, proofs of concept, prototypes), because it is unknown to both people and documents.

Stakeholders collaborate by participating during elicitation activities and by researching, studying, and providing feedback on documents, systems, models, and interfaces.

### Inputs

| Input | Source |
|---|---|
| **Elicitation Activity Plan** | Planned activities, techniques, logistics (date/time/location/resources/agenda), scope, and background information sources |

### Elements

1. **Guide Elicitation Activity** — Keep activities focused on producing intended information at the desired level of detail by considering: activity goals and agenda, scope of the change, output forms, what other representations results will support, how output integrates with existing knowledge, who provides and uses the information, and how it will be used. The BA also uses these to determine when sufficient elicitation has occurred and the activity can stop.

2. **Capture Elicitation Outcomes** — Elicitation is frequently iterative, occurring in parallel or sequential sessions. Unplanned outcomes are captured and integrated into appropriate planned outcomes. Recording ensures information is available for later reference and use.

### Guidelines and Tools

| Guideline/Tool | Role |
|---|---|
| **Business Analysis Approach** | Influences how each elicitation activity is performed; identifies needed output types |
| **Existing Business Analysis Information** | Guides questions and approach for drawing out information |
| **Stakeholder Engagement Approach** | Provides collaboration and communication approaches effective during elicitation |
| **Supporting Materials** | Any materials to prepare the BA and participants, plus information/tools/equipment used during elicitation |

### Techniques

- **Benchmarking and Market Analysis** — Compare processes, systems, products, or services with external baselines; determine customer wants and competitor offerings
- **Brainstorming** — Generate many ideas in a short period; organize and prioritize them
- **Business Rules Analysis** — Identify rules governing decisions that define, constrain, or enable operations
- **Collaborative Games** — Develop better understanding of problems or stimulate creative solutions
- **Concept Modelling** — Identify key terms and ideas of importance; define relationships between them
- **Data Mining** — Identify relevant information and patterns
- **Data Modelling** — Understand entity relationships during elicitation
- **Document Analysis** — Review existing systems, contracts, procedures, policies, standards, and regulations
- **Focus Groups** — Identify and understand ideas and attitudes from a group
- **Interface Analysis** — Understand interaction characteristics between two entities (systems, organizations, people/roles)
- **Interviews** — Ask questions to uncover needs, identify problems, or discover opportunities
- **Mind Mapping** — Generate many ideas; organize and prioritize them
- **Observation** — Gain insight about how work is currently done in different locations and circumstances
- **Process Analysis** — Understand current processes; identify improvement opportunities
- **Process Modelling** — Elicit processes with stakeholders during activities
- **Prototyping** — Elicit and validate needs through iterative model creation of requirements or designs
- **Survey or Questionnaire** — Elicit information (customers, products, work practices, attitudes) from groups in a structured, relatively fast way
- **Workshops** — Elicit information from groups in a collaborative, facilitated way

### Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Role |
|---|---|
| **Customer** | Provides valuable business analysis information during elicitation |
| **Domain Subject Matter Expert** | Provides required BA information; guides and assists in identifying research sources; helps arrange research, experiments, and facilitated elicitation |
| **End User** | User of existing and future solutions; should participate in elicitation |
| **Implementation Subject Matter Expert** | Designs/implments solutions; participates by asking clarifying questions and offering alternatives |
| **Sponsor** | Authorizes and ensures necessary stakeholders participate |
| **Any stakeholder** | May have relevant knowledge or experience to participate |

### Outputs

- **Elicitation Results (unconfirmed)** — Captured information in a format specific to the elicitation activity.

## 4.3 Confirm Elicitation Results

### Purpose

Check the information gathered during an elicitation session for accuracy and consistency with other information.

### Description

Elicited information is confirmed to identify and resolve problems before resources are committed to using it. This review may discover errors, omissions, conflicts, and ambiguity. Results are compared against their source and other elicitation results to ensure consistency. Collaboration with stakeholders may be needed to verify their inputs are correctly captured and that they agree with results of non-facilitated elicitation. Confirming is less rigorous and formal than analysis-phase review. If results are inconsistent, additional elicitation may be needed.

### Inputs

| Input | Source |
|---|---|
| **Elicitation Results (unconfirmed)** | Captured information in a format specific to the elicitation activity |

### Elements

1. **Compare Elicitation Results Against Source Information** — Lead follow-up meetings where stakeholders correct results, or stakeholders confirm independently. Sources include documents and stakeholder knowledge.

2. **Compare Elicitation Results Against Other Elicitation Results** — Compare results from multiple activities to confirm consistency and accurate representation. Identify variations and resolve with stakeholders. May also compare with historical data. Inconsistencies are often uncovered when developing specifications and models.

### Guidelines and Tools

| Guideline/Tool | Role |
|---|---|
| **Elicitation Activity Plan** | Guides which alternative sources and results should be compared |
| **Existing Business Analysis Information** | Confirms results or develops additional questions to draw out more detail |

### Techniques

- **Document Analysis** — Confirm results against source information or other existing documents
- **Interviews** — Confirm BA information and correctness of its integration
- **Reviews** — Confirm a set of elicitation results; can be informal or formal depending on risks of incorrect information
- **Workshops** — Conduct reviews of drafted results at any formality level; use predetermined agendas, scripts, or scenario tests with participant feedback

### Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Role |
|---|---|
| **Domain Subject Matter Expert** | Confirms results are correct; identifies omissions, inconsistencies, and conflicts; confirms the right information has been elicited |
| **Any stakeholder** | May need to participate in confirming elicitation results |

### Outputs

- **Elicitation Results (confirmed)** — Integrated output that the BA and stakeholders agree correctly reflects captured information and is relevant and useful as input to further work.

## 4.4 Communicate Business Analysis Information

### Purpose

Ensure stakeholders have a shared understanding of business analysis information.

### Description

Business analysts must communicate appropriate information to stakeholders at the right time and in formats that meet their needs, using language, tone, and style appropriate to the audience. Communication is bi-directional and iterative — it involves determining recipients, content, purpose, context, and expected outcomes. The BA engages stakeholders to ensure they understand and agree; if stakeholders are not receiving or understanding, the delivery method may need to change. Multiple forms of communication may be required for the same information.

### Inputs

| Input | Source |
|---|---|
| **Business Analysis Information** | Any information at any level of detail used as input or output of BA work; becomes an input when the need to communicate to additional stakeholders is discovered |
| **Stakeholder Engagement Approach** | Describes stakeholder groups, roles, and general communication needs |

### Elements

1. **Determine Objectives and Format of Communication** — Packages are prepared for reasons including: communicating requirements/designs, early quality assessment and planning, evaluating alternatives, formal reviews and approvals, inputs to solution design, regulatory/contractual conformance, and maintenance for reuse. Key questions: Who is the audience? What does each stakeholder type need? What is their preferred communication/learning style? What information is important? Is the format appropriate? How does it support other activities? Are there regulatory constraints?

   Possible forms: **Formal Documentation** (template-based, stable long-term record), **Informal Documentation** (used during change but not part of formal process), **Presentations** (high-level overview for decision making).

2. **Communicate Business Analysis Package** — Provide stakeholders the appropriate level of detail so they can understand, review, ask questions, and raise concerns. Communication platforms:
   - **Group collaboration** — Communicate to a group simultaneously; enables immediate discussion
   - **Individual collaboration** — One stakeholder at a time; useful when group setting is not feasible or productive
   - **E-mail or non-verbal methods** — Used when information maturity is high and little or no verbal explanation is needed

### Guidelines and Tools

| Guideline/Tool | Role |
|---|---|
| **Business Analysis Approach** | Describes how information will be disseminated: level of detail, formality, frequency, and communication effects of stakeholder number and geographic dispersion |
| **Information Management Approach** | Determines how BA information will be packaged and communicated |

### Techniques

- **Interviews** — Individually communicate information to stakeholders
- **Reviews** — Provide stakeholders opportunity to express feedback, request adjustments, understand required responses, and agree or approve; used in group or individual collaboration
- **Workshops** — Provide feedback opportunity, gain consensus, and provide approvals; typically used during group collaboration

### Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Role |
|---|---|
| **End User** | Needs frequent communication to stay aware of relevant BA information |
| **Customer** | Needs frequent communication to stay aware of relevant BA information |
| **Domain Subject Matter Expert** | Must understand BA information as part of confirming and validating it throughout the change |
| **Implementation Subject Matter Expert** | Must be aware of and understand BA information, particularly requirements and designs, for implementation |
| **Tester** | Must be aware of and understand BA information, particularly requirements and designs, for testing |
| **Any stakeholder** | Likely needs communication at some point during the change initiative |

### Outputs

- **Business Analysis Information (communicated)** — BA information considered communicated when target stakeholders have reached an understanding of its content and implications.

## 4.5 Manage Stakeholder Collaboration

### Purpose

Encourage stakeholders to work towards a common goal.

### Description

BA work naturally creates collaboration opportunities between stakeholder groups on work products. Stakeholders hold varying degrees of influence and authority and are important sources of needs, constraints, and assumptions. The BA identifies stakeholders, confirms roles, and communicates to ensure the right stakeholders participate at the right times in appropriate roles. This is ongoing — new stakeholders may emerge at any point, and each stakeholder's role, responsibility, influence, attitude, and authority may change over time.

The more significant and visible the change, the more attention managing collaboration requires. The BA capitalizes on positive reactions and mitigates or avoids negative ones, constantly monitoring each stakeholder's attitude.

Poor relationships can cause: failure to provide quality information, strong negative reactions to setbacks, resistance to change, lack of support and participation, and BA information being ignored. These are mitigated through strong, positive, trust-based relationships with stakeholders who provide services to the BA, depend on BA outputs, or participate in BA tasks.

### Inputs

| Input | Source |
|---|---|
| **Stakeholder Engagement Approach** | Describes types of expected engagement and how stakeholders may need to be managed |
| **Business Analysis Performance Assessment** | Provides information about effectiveness of BA tasks, including those focused on stakeholder engagement |

### Elements

1. **Gain Agreement on Commitments** — Stakeholders participate in activities requiring time and resource commitments. The BA and stakeholders identify and agree upon commitments as early as possible. Details can be communicated formally or informally, as long as there is explicit understanding of expectations and desired outcomes. May involve dialogue and negotiation; effective negotiation, communication, and conflict resolution skills are important.

2. **Monitor Stakeholder Engagement** — Monitor participation and performance to ensure: the right SMEs and stakeholders are participating effectively, attitudes and interest stay constant or improve, elicitation results are confirmed in a timely manner, and agreements/commitments are maintained. Continually monitor risks such as stakeholders diverted to other work, elicitation activities not providing quality information, and delayed approvals.

3. **Collaboration** — Stakeholders are more likely to support change when the BA collaborates with them and encourages the free flow of information, ideas, and innovations. Genuine engagement requires all stakeholders feel heard, their opinions matter, and contributions are recognized. Collaboration involves regular, frequent, bi-directional communication. Collaborative relationships maintain information flow when obstacles occur and promote shared effort to resolve problems.

### Guidelines and Tools

| Guideline/Tool | Role |
|---|---|
| **Business Analysis Approach** | Describes nature and level of collaboration required from each stakeholder group for planned BA activities |
| **Business Objectives** | Focus diverse stakeholders on a common vision of desired business outcomes |
| **Future State Description** | Defines desired future state and expected value; focuses stakeholders on the common goal |
| **Recommended Actions** | Communicating what should be done to improve solution value helps galvanize support and focus stakeholders |
| **Risk Analysis Results** | Stakeholder-related risks must be addressed to ensure collaboration activities succeed |

### Techniques

- **Collaborative Games** — Stimulate teamwork by temporarily immersing participants in safe, fun situations to share knowledge, identify hidden assumptions, and explore knowledge in ways that may not occur normally
- **Lessons Learned** — Understand stakeholders' satisfaction/dissatisfaction; offer them opportunity to help improve working relationships
- **Risk Analysis and Management** — Identify and manage risks related to stakeholder involvement, participation, and engagement
- **Stakeholder List, Map, or Personas** — Determine who is available to participate, show informal relationships between stakeholders, and understand which stakeholders to consult about different kinds of BA information

### Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Role |
|---|---|
| **All stakeholders** | All types of stakeholders who might be involved in collaboration during change |

### Outputs

- **Stakeholder Engagement** — Willingness from stakeholders to engage in BA activities and interact with the BA when necessary.
