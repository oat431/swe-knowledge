# 16 — Systems Engineering and Project Management

> *Source: SEBoK v2, Part 6 — Systems Engineering and Project Management*

## Purpose

Systems Engineering and Project Management are distinct but complementary disciplines. PM focuses on delivering the project within constraints (cost, schedule, resources), while SE focuses on delivering a system that meets stakeholder needs. Effective projects require strong collaboration between SE and PM.

## The Nature of Project Management

Project management is the application of knowledge, skills, tools, and techniques to project activities to meet project requirements. Key PM functions include:
- Scope, schedule, and cost management
- Resource allocation and optimization
- Risk management at the project level
- Stakeholder communication and reporting
- Procurement and contract management
- Quality management of project processes

## Overview of the PMBOK® Guide (in SE Context)

The PMBOK® Guide provides a framework of processes organized into performance domains. For systems engineers, the most relevant PMBOK areas are:
- **Integration Management** — Aligning SE activities with overall project plans
- **Scope Management** — Translating system requirements into project scope
- **Schedule Management** — Sequencing SE activities (reviews, analyses, integration events)
- **Risk Management** — Coordinating technical risk with project risk
- **Stakeholder Management** — Engaging technical and non-technical stakeholders

## Relationships between SE and PM

The SE and PM relationship operates along several dimensions:

| Dimension | SE Focus | PM Focus |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| **Primary Concern** | Technical integrity of the system | Project delivery within constraints |
| **Success Criteria** | System meets requirements and stakeholder needs | On time, on budget, to specification |
| **Risk Focus** | Technical risk (performance, interfaces, technology maturity) | Project risk (schedule, cost, resources) |
| **Decision Authority** | Technical decisions (architecture, design, verification) | Business decisions (funding, staffing, contracting) |
| **Planning Horizon** | Full system life cycle (development through disposal) | Project life cycle (initiation through closure) |

Key integration points: Work Breakdown Structure (WBS), Integrated Master Schedule (IMS), Technical Performance Measures (TPMs), risk register, and decision gates/reviews.

## Influence of Project Structure and Governance

The organizational relationship between SE and PM significantly affects outcomes:
- **SE reports to PM** — Common in smaller projects; PM controls all resources
- **SE and PM as peers** — Balanced approach for complex systems; requires strong collaboration
- **SE as independent authority** — Common in safety-critical or highly regulated domains; SE has veto power on technical decisions
- **IPPD/IPT structures** — Integrated Product Teams with co-leadership from SE and PM

## Cost Estimating and Analysis in SE

Systems engineers contribute to cost estimation through:
- **Technical baseline definition** — What are we building? Defines the scope for cost estimation
- **Parametric estimation** — Using historical data and system parameters (weight, lines of code, complexity)
- **Life cycle cost analysis** — Considering development, production, operations, support, and disposal
- **Trade studies** — Balancing cost against performance, schedule, and risk
- **Earned Value Management (EVM)** — SE contributes technical progress assessment to EVM

## Procurement and Acquisition

SE plays a critical role in procurement:
- Defining technical requirements for RFPs and contracts
- Evaluating supplier technical proposals
- Managing technical interfaces between suppliers
- Overseeing supplier technical performance
- Integrating supplier deliverables into the system

## Portfolio Management

At the portfolio level, SE contributes to:
- Capability gap analysis and investment prioritization
- Technical interoperability across projects
- Common architecture and standards enforcement
- Resource and technology sharing across projects
- Balancing near-term delivery with long-term capability evolution
